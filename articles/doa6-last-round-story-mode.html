DOA6 Story Mode Guide: Complete Walkthrough, All Rewards & How Long to Beat

Dead or Alive 6 Story Mode weaves together multiple character perspectives across the sixth Dead or Alive tournament. Whether you are here for the narrative, the unlockable rewards, or just to clear every chapter as fast as possible, this guide covers every episode, every reward, and the fastest route through the entire campaign.

Story Mode Overview: What to Expect

DOA6 Story Mode is a narrative-driven single-player campaign told through 12 main episodes plus bonus chapters, totaling over 100 individual scenes. Unlike traditional fighting game arcade modes, DOA6 tells its story through interconnected character perspectives — you switch between multiple fighters as the plot advances, experiencing key events from different angles.

Player Points (PP) Earnings

Each scene awards between 100 and 500 PP depending on scene length and difficulty. Completing all main episodes typically yields around 28,000 to 35,000 PP, and adding bonus chapters pushes that to approximately 40,000 to 45,000 PP. This currency can be spent in the in-game shop on additional costume parts, music tracks, and gallery items.

How to Complete Story Mode Fast

If your goal is rewards rather than narrative, these strategies will cut your completion time significantly:

  1. Skip cutscenes aggressively. Press the Options/Menu button during any cutscene to bring up the skip prompt. Every scene has a skip option — use it on scenes you have already seen or do not care about.
  2. Lower the difficulty to Rookie. Story Mode fights are not difficulty-locked. Setting the game to Rookie difficulty makes every fight trivial — the AI barely blocks and almost never counters. You can clear combat scenes in under 30 seconds.
  3. Use a fast, high-damage character. Kasumi and Hitomi are excellent choices for speed-clearing Story Mode fights. Kasumi's quick strings and Hitomi's high damage output from simple combos end fights fast. Avoid grapplers like Bass or Bayman for Story Mode speed runs — their damage takes more setup.
  4. Skip the bonus chapters on the first pass. Bonus chapters do not block any achievements or major rewards. Complete the 12 main episodes first to unlock the core costume parts, then circle back for bonus content later if you want the "Story Master" title.
  5. One-sitting estimate. On Rookie difficulty with cutscenes skipped, you can finish all 12 main episodes in approximately 3 to 4 hours.
FASTEST ROUTE: Rookie difficulty + Kasumi + skip all cutscenes = main story cleared in ~3 hours. Target Episodes 1, 2, 3, 6, and 12 first for costume parts, then clean up the rest.

Story Summary & Key Plot Points (Spoiler-Light)

DOA6 takes place after the events of Dead or Alive 5. The sixth Dead or Alive tournament is announced, but behind the public spectacle, a deeper conspiracy unfolds. Here are the key plot threads without heavy spoilers:

  • The MIST Organization: A shadowy scientific group led by NiCO pursues forbidden research involving human augmentation. Their experiments connect directly to Honoka's mysterious power and the existence of Phase 4, the Kasumi clone.
  • Helena's Investigation: As head of the reborn DOATEC, Helena Douglas uses the tournament as a front to investigate MIST. She works alongside Marie Rose, whose loyalty to Helena runs deeper than initially apparent.
  • The Ninja Conflict: Kasumi is hunted by her own clan while simultaneously being targeted by MIST for her unique genetic code. Hayabusa and Hayate form an uneasy alliance to protect her, while Ayane struggles between duty to the clan and loyalty to her half-sister.
  • Diego's Rise: A street fighter from New York, Diego enters the tournament seeking to prove himself. His storyline serves as the newcomer's lens through which players experience the tournament's scale.
  • Honoka's Secret: The cheerful schoolgirl possesses a power she does not fully understand — one that connects her to both the Mugen Tenshin clan and MIST's experiments in ways that become central to the final act.

Story Mode vs. DOA Quest: What is the Difference?

New players often confuse these two modes. Here is the clear distinction:

RECOMMENDATION: Play Story Mode first for the narrative and easy PP. Then tackle DOA Quest missions as a training tool — the specific combat objectives (land 3-hit combos, perform holds, etc.) directly improve your competitive skills.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is DOA6 Story Mode?
DOA6 Story Mode takes approximately 8 to 12 hours to complete all main episodes at normal difficulty with cutscenes watched. Completionists aiming for 100% including bonus chapters can expect 15 to 20 hours. On Rookie difficulty with cutscenes skipped, you can finish the main story in about 3 to 4 hours.
Does DOA6 Story Mode unlock any characters?
No. In both the original DOA6 and DOA6 Last Round, all characters are available from the start. Story Mode completion is not required to unlock any fighter. The 24 base characters and all 7 DLC characters (in Last Round) are immediately selectable.
What rewards do you get from DOA6 Story Mode?
Story Mode rewards include costume parts for characters like Kasumi, Honoka, Diego, NiCO, Phase 4, Hayabusa, and Helena, as well as player titles and Player Points (PP). Completing all 12 main episodes awards the "DOA Champion" title, and 100% completion including bonus chapters awards "Story Master."
How many chapters are in DOA6 Story Mode?
DOA6 Story Mode contains 12 main story episodes plus bonus chapters. The total number of individual scenes exceeds 100. Each episode focuses on different characters — the full breakdown is in the Episode Structure table above.
What is the difference between Story Mode and DOA Quest?
Story Mode is a narrative-driven linear campaign with cutscenes and fixed character assignments. DOA Quest is a mission-based mode where you complete specific combat objectives (e.g., land 3-hit combos, perform holds) to earn rewards. Story Mode is about experiencing the plot; DOA Quest is a training tool with unlockable incentives.
Is Story Mode worth playing in DOA6?
Yes, for three reasons: it awards unique costume parts available nowhere else, the PP earnings help fund in-game shop purchases, and for players interested in character lore it provides context that makes the roster more meaningful. Even competitive-focused players benefit from the free rewards.
Can you skip cutscenes in DOA6 Story Mode?
Yes. Press the Options/Menu button during any cutscene to open the pause menu, then select Skip. Every cutscene in Story Mode can be skipped. There is no penalty for skipping — you still receive all rewards for completing the scene.
Does difficulty affect Story Mode rewards?
No. Story Mode rewards are fixed per episode regardless of difficulty setting. You earn the same costume parts, titles, and PP on Rookie difficulty as you do on Legend. There is no bonus for completing Story Mode on higher difficulties.
