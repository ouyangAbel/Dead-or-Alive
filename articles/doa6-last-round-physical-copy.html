PS5 Physical Edition — Full Blu-ray Release

The PlayStation 5 version of Dead or Alive 6 Last Round ships on a single Blu-ray disc that contains the complete game. This isn't one of those frustrating "disc is just a license key" situations — the full 30+ GB base game is on the disc, including all post-launch DLC characters, costumes, and stages that were originally released as paid add-ons for the base DOA6.

When you insert the PS5 disc, you'll go through a standard installation process that takes roughly 8–12 minutes depending on your drive speed. After that, the game may prompt a small day-one patch (typically under 500 MB) that addresses minor bug fixes and online stability improvements. But critically, all content — fighters, stages, costumes, and game modes — is accessible from the disc alone without any internet connection.

What's in the PS5 Box?

Depending on which edition you purchase, here's what you'll find inside:

  • Standard Edition ($49.99 MSRP): Blu-ray disc in a standard PS5 case, reversible cover art featuring the full roster, and a quick-start guide. All DLC content is included on the disc.
  • Collector's Edition ($99.99 MSRP): Everything from the Standard Edition, plus a steelbook case with exclusive Kasumi & Ayane artwork, a 64-page mini art book covering character designs and stage concept art, and a digital soundtrack download code. Quantities are limited — Koei Tecmo confirmed this is a single-print-run edition.
⚡ PRO TIP: If you care about resale value, the Collector's Edition is the way to go. Koei Tecmo fighting game steelbooks historically appreciate 30–50% within 2 years of going out of print, especially DOA titles with Kasumi cover art.

Nintendo Switch 2 Physical Edition — Cartridge Confirmed

The Nintendo Switch 2 physical release is arguably the most exciting option for collectors, simply because DOA6 has never been on a Nintendo platform before. The Last Round marks the series' debut on Nintendo hardware, and the physical cartridge is a genuine piece of fighting game history.

The Switch 2 cartridge contains the complete game plus all DLC, pre-loaded on the card. No additional SD card download is required for content — the entire package fits on the cartridge. Loading times from the Switch 2 cart are impressively fast (approximately 5–8 seconds to boot into a match), comparable to internal storage speeds thanks to the Switch 2's improved cartridge read architecture.

Switch 2 Physical Advantages

  • Region-free: The Switch 2 inherits the region-free cartridge compatibility of its predecessor. A Japanese copy works on a North American Switch 2, and vice versa. This is huge for collectors who want specific regional box art or first-print bonuses.
  • No install required: Unlike the PS5 disc, the Switch 2 cartridge doesn't require a mandatory install. Pop it in and play within seconds. This makes it ideal for players with limited internal storage.
  • Portability: This is the first time you can play DOA6 in handheld mode with a physical cartridge. Take it to locals, tournaments, or just play on the couch without needing a disc drive.
💡 DID YOU KNOW? Japanese first-print Switch 2 copies of DOA6 Last Round include a bonus reversible cover with exclusive Marie Rose and Honoka artwork not available in Western editions. If you're importing, look for the "初回生産版" (First Print Edition) badge on the packaging.

Xbox Series X|S — Digital Only (What Happened?)

This is the question that frustrates a lot of Xbox players: why no physical Xbox release? The short answer is economics. Physical game manufacturing for Xbox has been declining across the industry, and Japanese publishers in particular have been moving away from Xbox disc production. Koei Tecmo's internal data likely showed that the cost of manufacturing, distributing, and warehousing Xbox discs for DOA6 Last Round wouldn't be justified by projected sales.

Microsoft's own push toward digital with the Xbox Series S (which has no disc drive at all) and the all-digital Xbox Series X refresh has accelerated this trend. For a niche fighting game like DOA6, the publisher faces an increasingly unfavorable cost-benefit calculation for Xbox physical media.

If you're on Xbox and want a physical copy, your practical options are:

  • Buy the PS5 or Switch 2 physical edition as a collector's item and play the digital version on Xbox.
  • Wait and hope — though Koei Tecmo has given zero indication of an Xbox physical release, and fighting games rarely receive physical editions post-launch if they weren't planned from the start.

Our Recommendation

If you're a DOA fan or fighting game collector, get the physical edition — specifically the Switch 2 cartridge for its historic significance as the series' first Nintendo physical release, or the PS5 Collector's Edition for the steelbook and art book. If you're a competitive player who just wants to grind ranked matches, the digital version is cheaper, more convenient, and you won't have to swap discs between sessions. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Dead or Alive 6 Last Round getting a physical release?
Yes. Dead or Alive 6 Last Round is confirmed to have physical copies on PS5 (Blu-ray disc) and Nintendo Switch 2 (game cartridge). Both physical editions include the full base game plus all DLC characters, costumes, and stages on the physical media — no additional downloads required beyond a small day-one patch. The Xbox Series X|S and PC versions are digital-only with no physical options available or planned.
Q: Will DOA6 Last Round get a physical release on Xbox?
No. As of July 2026, Dead or Alive 6 Last Round is digital-only on Xbox Series X|S and Xbox One. Koei Tecmo has not announced any plans for an Xbox physical edition, and given the industry-wide decline of Xbox physical game manufacturing — especially for Japanese fighting games — a future physical Xbox release is extremely unlikely. Xbox players who want a physical copy should consider purchasing the PS5 or Switch 2 version alongside their digital Xbox copy.
Q: Is the Switch 2 physical cartridge region-locked?
No. The Nintendo Switch 2, like the original Switch, is not region-locked for physical game cartridges. You can purchase a Japanese, North American, European, or any other regional copy of DOA6 Last Round and it will work on any Switch 2 console worldwide. The game will automatically detect your system language and adjust menus accordingly. However, DLC codes included in special or first-print editions are region-locked to their respective eShop regions — a Japanese costume code requires a Japanese Nintendo Account to redeem, though the unlocked content can then be used by any account on the same console.
Q: Does the physical copy include all DLC on the disc or cartridge?
Yes. Both the PS5 Blu-ray disc and Switch 2 cartridge contain the full game plus all DLC content on the physical media. This includes every post-launch character — Mai Shiranui, Kula Diamond, Tamaki, Momiji, and Rachel — as well as all costume packs and stages that were originally released as paid DLC for the base DOA6. You do not need an internet connection to access any of this content. A small day-one patch (typically under 500 MB) may download automatically to address minor bug fixes and online stability improvements, but the entire content library is present and playable directly from the disc or cartridge.
Q: Where can I buy the DOA6 Last Round physical copy?
The PS5 and Switch 2 physical editions are available at all major North American retailers including Amazon, GameStop, Best Buy, Walmart, and Target, as well as directly from the Koei Tecmo online store. European buyers can find copies at GAME (UK), FNAC (France), MediaMarkt (Germany), and Amazon EU. For Japanese import copies with exclusive first-print bonuses, Play-Asia and Amazon Japan offer international shipping. The standard edition retails for $49.99 USD / €49.99 / £44.99, while the Collector's Edition is priced at $99.99 USD / €99.99 / £89.99. The Collector's Edition is a single-print-run product — once it sells out, it will not be restocked.
Q: Is the physical copy worth buying over the digital version?
The physical copy is absolutely worth it if you value ownership, want to resell or trade the game later, have limited internet bandwidth, or are a collector. The PS5 disc and Switch 2 cartridge contain all DLC on the media, meaning the game remains fully playable even if digital storefronts eventually shut down — which is a real long-term concern for fighting games with licensed guest characters (Mai and Kula are SNK properties with time-limited licensing). However, the digital version offers instant access with no disc-swapping, is typically $5–$10 cheaper during launch-week promotions, and is the only option if you play on Xbox or PC. For most DOA fans, we recommend the physical Switch 2 cartridge as the best overall value — it combines portability, collector appeal as the series' Nintendo debut, and true offline ownership.
