DOA6 Last Round Kasumi Guide: Combos, Frame Data & Complete Strategy for Mastering the Mugen Tenshin Kunoichi

Kasumi is the face of Dead or Alive — and for good reason. Kasumi Overview: Playstyle, Strengths & Weaknesses

Kasumi is a rushdown speed character who thrives at close-to-mid range. Her game plan is straightforward but demanding: use her fastest-in-class pokes to control neutral, condition the opponent to block, then open them up with Hoshinpo teleport mix-ups or counter-hit launchers. Once she lands a launch, her wall carry and juggle damage are among the best of the speed archetype.

Strengths

  • Fastest neutral control: 9-frame jab, 12-frame mid (6P), and 14-frame low (3K) give her the best poke game of any character. She can interrupt strings that other characters must block.
  • Unpredictable approach: Hoshinpo teleport dash lets her close distance from half-screen in under 20 frames, creating strike/throw/low mix-ups that are extremely difficult to react to.
  • Strong wall game: 6PK is one of the best wall carry strings in DOA6. On stages like The Throwdown, Kasumi can convert virtually any hit into a wall combo.
  • High-damage Break Blow routes: With full meter, her Break Blow wall extension deals 140+ damage — among the highest for speed characters.

Weaknesses

  • Glass cannon: 950 HP means she dies fast. Two optimized combos from a power character like Bass or Raidou can end the round.
  • Linear offense: 33K, 6PK, and 6P+K all have poor tracking. A patient opponent who sidesteps at the right time will shut down her approach.
  • Meter-dependent damage: Without Break Gauge, her midscreen combo damage is below average (70-90). You need to manage meter carefully to access her best routes.
  • Weak throws: No launch throws or combo throws. Hoshinpo~T is her only mix-up throw and it resets to neutral rather than leading to combo damage.
PRO TIP: Kasumi's game plan can be summarized as "poke until they crack." Your goal is not to land one big launcher — it is to chip away with safe pokes (6P, 3K, 2P) until the opponent becomes impatient and presses a button. That is when you land your counter-hit 6P or 33K and convert into 120+ damage. Patience is the hidden Kasumi skill that separates average players from mains.

Hoshinpo Teleport: The Complete Dash & Cancel Guide

Hoshinpo (literally "Rushing Step") is the mechanic that separates Kasumi from every other speed character. It is a forward teleport dash with four follow-up options and a cancel. Understanding when to use each follow-up — and, critically, when to cancel — is what makes Kasumi terrifying at high levels.

The Hoshinpo Cancel Game

The Hoshinpo cancel (input 66 then immediately press H) is Kasumi's most powerful conditioning tool. When you dash at the opponent and cancel, you create a zero-commitment approach — you are close enough to threaten strike/throw but you never actually committed to an attack. Here is how to build your offense around it:

  1. Round start: Hoshinpo cancel at round start to gauge the opponent's reaction. Did they press a button? Did they hold? Did they backdash? Take a mental note.
  2. After a knockdown: Hoshinpo into the opponent's wake-up range and cancel. They must guess between Hoshinpo~K (low), Hoshinpo~T (throw), or you cancel and use normal 6P. This is a true three-way mix-up.
  3. Feint into whiff punish: Hoshinpo cancel → wait for opponent's panic hold/attack → whiff punish with 33K. This sequence alone will win you rounds against impatient players.
PRO TIP: The single most important Hoshinpo rhythm to learn: 66H (cancel) → micro-walk forward → throw. The cancel animation recovery is fast enough that you can throw immediately after. Most opponents see the dash and freeze, expecting a strike. Throw them instead. This works at every rank.

Wall Carry & Wall Combo Optimization

Kasumi is one of the best wall-carry characters in DOA6 Last Round. Her 6PK string pushes opponents horizontally with every hit, and Hoshinpo mid-combo can close the final distance to guarantee a wall splat. Here are the stage-specific routes you need.

Universal Wall Carry Principle

Every Kasumi wall combo follows this template: launcher → carry string → splat move → wall ender. The carry string is almost always 6PK plus any forward-dash attacks needed. The splat move is usually P+K (fast, high-knockback mid) or the second hit of 6PK if you are already close to the wall.

Stage-Specific Tips

  • The Throwdown (Octagon): Walls on all sides. Always use your wall route by default — you will reach a wall within 2-3 hits from any position. Favor launcher → 6PK → P+K → PP6PK for consistency.
  • Zero (Lab): Close walls plus electric floors. If you launch near the electric panels, angle your 6PK to carry toward the nearest wall. Electric floor launches give you a second wall splat opportunity for 160+ damage sequences.
  • Forbidden Fortune (Ship): Side rails are close. Launch → 6PK → 66P → the rail counts as a wall splat. Break Blow on this stage can send opponents over the rail for a stage transition.
  • Chinese Festival (Open): Walls only at far edges. Do not fish for wall combos here — use your midscreen routes and save Break Blow for guaranteed damage rather than carry attempts.
WALL AWARENESS DRILL: Load into Training Mode on The Throwdown. Start center stage. Launch with 33K and count how many 6PK hits you need to reach the wall. The answer should be exactly two (33K → 6PK → 6PK second hit = wall splat). This is your "carry count" and you should know it for every launcher, on every stage.

Break Blow & Break Hold Kasumi-Specific Routes

Kasumi's Break Blow is one of her best tools. With full Break Gauge, her combo damage jumps from the 80-90 range to the 130-150 range. The Break Hold (4S) is also excellent — it gives her a defensive reversal that speed characters typically lack.

Break Blow Wall Extension (Optimal)

The sequence: 33K (launcher) → 6S (Break Blow, hold S to charge) → opponent hits wall (wall splat) → press H to cancel → 6PK → PP6PK. Total damage: ~140. This is Kasumi's highest-damage route and should be your goal whenever you have full meter near a wall.

Cancel timing is the hardest part — press H roughly 15 frames after the wall impact sound. If you cancel too early, the splat does not register and the opponent falls normally. Too late, and they slide down before your 6PK connects. Practice in Training Mode with Critical Gauge set to FULL until you can land 10 consecutive cancel extensions.

Break Hold Applications

Kasumi's Break Hold (4S) is a universal parry that beats all strike levels (high, mid, low). Use it in these situations:

  • On wake-up: When the opponent runs a predictable meaty pressure sequence, Break Hold on wake-up beats their attack and resets neutral.
  • Against string pressure: Characters like Hitomi and Jann Lee have long, delayable strings. If you recognize the gap, Break Hold during the delay window to steal momentum.
  • As a round-ender: If the opponent has low health, a raw Break Hold into Break Blow can surprise-kill. The Break Hold animation is fast enough that many players do not react.
METER PRIORITY: As Kasumi, prioritize Break Blow combos over Break Holds. Your health is too low to rely on defensive reads — offensive meter usage (wall extensions, mid-combo Break Blows) closes out rounds faster and reduces the number of interactions where your low HP can be exploited.

Matchup Strategy: How to Fight Every Archetype

Kasumi's matchup spread is generally favorable against slow characters and even-to-unfavorable against other fast characters. Here is the archetype-by-archetype game plan.

Vs. Grapplers (Bass, Tina, Leon, Bayman)

Favorable. Grapplers cannot keep up with your speed. The game plan: constant movement and 2P interrupts. Never stand still at throw range — grappler command throws out-damage your entire combo. Use 2P (12-frame low jab) to beat their throw startups, then convert into 3K pressure. Key move: 2P → 3K → 3KK (on hit). This low-to-low sequence beats both stand blocking and throw attempts.

The danger: if a grappler reads your Hoshinpo approach, a single command throw can deal 80-120 damage — more than a third of your health bar. Do not Hoshinpo at close range against grapplers. Use it only from mid-range to close distance, and always cancel into block (66H → hold back) if you see them moving forward.

Vs. Speed Characters (Ayane, Christie, Hitomi, Phase 4)

Even. These matchups are decided by who controls the pace. Against other speed characters, Kasumi's 9-frame jab is still the fastest button, but their own fast pokes can challenge it on counter-hit. Key strategy: play slightly more defensive. Let them approach and use your 6P at max range to stuff their dash-ins. Kasumi's 6P beats most speed characters' approach tools because of its 12-frame startup and forward hitbox.

Against Ayane specifically: beware her spinning evasion. Ayane's back-turned stance and spins can evade your linear 6P and 33K. In this matchup, favor 3K (low) and 2P (crouch jab) to catch her during spins, and use Hoshinpo~T more than usual since Ayane players tend to hold frequently.

Vs. All-Rounders (Hayabusa, Jann Lee, Rig, Diego)

Slightly unfavorable. These characters have the tools to match your speed while out-damaging you. Hayabusa's Izuna and Jann Lee's Dragon Kick can delete your health bar in two interactions. Key strategy: safe pressure only. Do not overextend — use 6P and 3K as your only pressure tools and end every string with a safe move (do not finish 6PK on block unless you confirm the hit).

Against Hayabusa: respect his 8K launcher (same speed as your 33K, but leads to more damage). Do not use 4H+K against him — his standing kicks will crush your low-crush attempt. Against Jann Lee: his 66K (Dragon Kick) will beat your Hoshinpo approach from mid-range. Bait it with a Hoshinpo cancel (66H) and whiff-punish with 33K.

Vs. Heavy Zoners (Raidou, Nyotengu, Helena)

Favorable. These characters want distance. Kasumi wants to be in their face. Use Hoshinpo to close the gap quickly and stay at range 0. Once you are in, their slower buttons cannot compete with your 9-frame jab. Key move: Hoshinpo cancel → instant 6P. This approach-to-immediate-poke sequence is faster than anything they can do at close range. Once they start blocking, layer the Hoshinpo~K low to open them up.

MATCHUP NOTEBOOK: After every ranked session, write down one matchup insight. Example: "Bass players always command throw after blocking 6P — next time I will 2P after 6P on block instead of continuing the string." Over 20 sessions, you will have custom counter-play for every character on the roster.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Kasumi good in DOA6 Last Round?
Yes, Kasumi is a solid A-tier character in DOA6 Last Round. She has the fastest strike speed in the game (9-frame jab), excellent mobility through her teleport dashes (Hoshinpo), strong wall carry, and high-damage Break Blow combos. Her main weakness is low health (950 HP) and reliance on landing counter-hit launchers for her best damage. In the right hands, she is one of the most dangerous rushdown characters in the game and sees consistent tournament representation at all levels.
Q: What are Kasumi's best combo starters in DOA6?
Kasumi's best combo starters are 33K (15-frame mid launcher, safe on block — your go-to whiff punisher), 8K (17-frame high launcher for anti-air situations), 6P+K (16-frame mid launcher with Hoshinpo transition for extended juggles), and 4H+K (18-frame low-crushing launcher that beats throw attempts). For counter-hit fishing, her 6P (12-frame mid) and 3K (14-frame low) are the two buttons you will press most often in neutral.
Q: How do I use Kasumi's teleport (Hoshinpo) effectively?
Kasumi's Hoshinpo is her signature mobility tool with four follow-up options: Hoshinpo~P (safe mid poke, +1 on block), Hoshinpo~K (unseeable low, full combo on counter-hit), Hoshinpo~T (command grab that beats blocking), and Hoshinpo cancel (press H during dash to feint). The key to effective Hoshinpo usage is the dash cancel mix-up — approach with 66H (cancel) and then either throw immediately, use 6P, or wait to whiff-punish their panic reaction. Use Hoshinpo~K sparingly (once or twice per round) so the opponent always fears the low. Overusing any Hoshinpo option makes you predictable and punishable.
Q: What is Kasumi's best wall combo route?
Kasumi's optimal wall combo on The Throwdown is 33K (launcher) → 6PK (wall carry) → P+K (wall splat) → PP6PK (wall ender), dealing approximately 120-130 damage. With full Break Gauge: 33K → Break Blow (hold S, wall splat) → cancel with H → 6PK → PP6PK for 140-150 damage. The key to Kasumi wall combos is using 6PK as the carry tool — it has strong horizontal knockback and the second hit naturally wall splats on close-wall stages.
Q: How do I deal with grapplers as Kasumi?
Against grapplers like Bass and Tina, Kasumi must leverage her superior speed and movement. Never stand still at throw range — use constant backdash cancels and 2P (12-frame low jab) to interrupt throw attempts. Kasumi's 6P beats most command throw startups with its 12-frame speed. Key sequence: 2P (interrupt their throw) → 3K (low poke, +2 on hit) → 6P (counter-hit fish as they try to take their turn back) → 33K combo. Avoid raw Hoshinpo at close range against grapplers — if they read the teleport, a command throw punish deals more damage than your entire combo.
Q: What are Kasumi's weaknesses and bad matchups in DOA6?
Kasumi's three main weaknesses are: low health (950 HP — dies in 2-3 fewer hits than average), linear offense (her best moves like 6PK and 33K have poor tracking, making her vulnerable to sidestep), and meter dependency (without Break Gauge her damage drops to 70-90 per combo). Her worst matchups are against characters with strong defensive tools: Hayabusa (Izuna punishes her rushdown, and his whiff punishes out-damage her), Jann Lee (Dragon Kick beats Hoshinpo approach, and his damage-per-hit is much higher), and Bayman (his tank roll and offensive holds shut down her predictable string pressure).
