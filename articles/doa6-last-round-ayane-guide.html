DOA6 Last Round Ayane Guide: Combos, Spinning Stance, Juggle Routes & Complete Strategy

Ayane — the shadow kunoichi of the Mugen Tenshin clan, the half-sister of Kasumi, and one of the most unique setplay-evasion hybrids in Dead or Alive 6 Last Round — is a character who defies conventional fighting game archetypes. She does not rush you down like Hitomi, does not zone you out like a traditional projectile character, and does not grapple you into submission like Bass. Instead, Ayane dances at the edge of your effective range, spinning through your attacks with her signature evasion stances, and punishing every whiff with devastating back-turned juggle routes. This guide covers everything from her fundamental spinning stance mechanics to advanced Hajin-Ryu setplay, providing the complete path from your first BT 4K launcher to tournament-ready Ayane play.

Ayane Overview: Playstyle, Strengths & Weaknesses

Ayane is a setplay-evasion hybrid who thrives on making the opponent miss and then punishing with high-damage juggles from her back-turned (BT) stance. Her game plan is built around three core systems: the spinning stance (4P, 1P+K, 214P — three distinct evasion tools that transition her into back-turned with frame advantage), the back-turned stance offense (a complete secondary move list with its own launchers, pokes, throws, and Break Blow options), and post-knockdown okizeme (layered pressure sequences from BT that force the opponent to guess between mid launchers, low pokes, command throws, and delayed pressure resets).

Strengths

  • Unmatched evasion: 4P spin has evasion starting at frame 4 — faster than most jabs. 1P+K high-crushes at frame 1. No other character in DOA6 has access to evasion tools this fast that also transition into offensive stance with frame advantage.
  • Back-turned stance depth: BT Ayane has a 40+ move list separate from her frontal kit, including launchers (BT 4K, BT 8K), throws (BT T, BT 6T), tracking moves (BT 6P), and armored options (BT 4H+K Break Blow). Opponents must learn two completely different defense patterns — one for frontal Ayane, one for BT Ayane.
  • Best okizeme in the game: After a knockdown, BT Ayane can threaten BT 4K (mid launcher), BT 2K (low), BT T (throw), BT 6P (safe mid pressure), or delayed BT 4H+K (armored Break Blow). Five distinct threats from a single wake-up situation — no other character creates this much ambiguity on okizeme.
  • Positional control: Her spinning stance doesn't just evade — it repositions. 4P spins Ayane roughly one character width to the side. 1P+K spins her forward and low. 214P covers significant distance. This means Ayane can control not just whether she gets hit, but where she ends up on the screen, enabling corner escapes and ring positioning that other characters cannot replicate.
  • Strong wall carry: Ayane's juggle routes carry corner-to-corner on most DOA6 stages, and her wall-splat conversions lead to some of the highest wall damage in the game thanks to BT 4H+K follow-ups.

Weaknesses

  • Linear attacks: Ayane's best pokes (6P, 3K, BT 6P) and her spin entries are all linear. A patient opponent who sidesteps at the right moment will make Ayane's neutral game feel suffocatingly narrow, forcing her to commit to slower tracking moves (like 1P+K or 3H+K) that can be whiff-punished.
  • BT throw vulnerability: When Ayane is in BT stance, she cannot break throws. If the opponent reads a BT transition and throws immediately, the throw is guaranteed. This is the single biggest counterplay against Ayane — every BT entry at close range carries the risk of eating a free throw, which for grapplers like Bass or Tina can mean 60-80 damage.
  • Height-dependent juggles: Unlike characters with universal combo routes, Ayane's juggle enders change based on launcher height, opponent weight class, and counter-hit status. You need to learn 3-4 different juggle enders and recognize which one to use mid-combo, which adds a layer of mental load that characters like Hitomi or Kasumi do not have.
  • Stubby close-range options: At point-blank range, Ayane's fastest buttons (6P at i12, 2P at i13) are slightly slower than the i9-i11 jabs of characters like Kasumi, Christie, or Hitomi. In scramble situations, Ayane loses to faster characters and must use 4P spin to escape rather than contesting directly.
PRO TIP: The number one mistake new Ayane players make is spinning into BT at unsafe ranges. 4P and 1P+K are evasion tools — they should be used when you predict an attack, not when you want to access BT stance. If you want to enter BT stance safely offensively, use 214P from mid-range (where the spin covers distance and the transition is protected by range) or enter BT after a hard knockdown (where the opponent cannot throw you during the transition because they are getting up). Think of your spins as defensive counters first and stance entries second.

Understanding the BT Advantage System

When Ayane transitions into BT stance via any of her spin moves, she gains frame advantage that varies by the spin used. This advantage determines what the opponent can do during your BT transition:

  • 1P+K spin: Transitions to BT at +5. This means after the spin, your BT 4K (i15) effectively becomes i10 — faster than any normal in the game. If the opponent presses any button after your 1P+K spin, BT 4K will counter-hit them. This is Ayane's most powerful offensive transition.
  • 4P spin: Transitions to BT at +3. BT 4K becomes effectively i12, which beats most pokes but loses to i9-i10 jabs from the fastest characters. Use BT 6P (effectively i10 at +3) if the opponent has a very fast jab.
  • 214P spin: Transitions to BT at +2 on block. BT 4K becomes effectively i13, which is contestable. On hit, the advantage is larger and BT 4K is guaranteed as a combo — this is why 214P on hit is so dangerous.
DRILL THIS: Set Training Mode dummy to "Guard All" and "Attack after Guarding" with a 12-frame mid. Practice 1P+K (duck under the dummy's attack) → BT 4K. The goal is to launch the dummy on every successful duck. If the dummy blocks your BT 4K, you either spun too early or pressed BT 4K too late. This single drill will build the muscle memory for Ayane's most fundamental sequence: evade → punish.

Spinning Stance Mastery: 4P, 1P+K, 214P & Evasion Windows

Ayane's three spin moves — 4P, 1P+K, and 214P — are superficially similar but serve fundamentally different purposes. Understanding which spin to use in which situation is the skill that separates competent Ayane players from expert ones.

4P: The Defensive Spin

Input: 4P (back + P). Evasion: Starts at frame 4, active until frame 19. Transition to BT: +3 advantage.

4P is your primary defensive tool. At 4 frames of evasion startup, it goes under the startup of almost every move in the game. Use 4P when:

  • The opponent is pressing buttons after your blocked moves (frame-trap counterplay).
  • You read a linear approach tool (Jann Lee's Dragon Kick, Hayabusa's 7K, Kasumi's 66P).
  • You are at slight disadvantage (-2 to -4) and expect the opponent to continue pressure with a linear mid.

The key to 4P is not overusing it. If you spin every time you are at -2, good opponents will start using tracking strings or delayed buttons to catch your spin recovery. 4P should be used as a read, not a reflex. The threat of 4P is often more valuable than 4P itself — opponents who know you have a frame-4 evasion tool will hesitate to press buttons, giving you passive frame advantage even when you are technically minus.

1P+K: The High-Crush Spin

Input: 1P+K (down-forward + P+K). Evasion: High-crush starting at frame 1 (instant). Transition to BT: +5 advantage — Ayane's most advantageous BT transition.

1P+K is the ultimate high-crush tool. At frame 1, it goes under high attacks literally before they start. Use 1P+K when:

  • Opponents are using high-heavy pressure strings (Kasumi's jab pressure, Christie's high strings).
  • You predict a throw attempt — throws are treated as highs and 1P+K goes under them at frame 1.
  • You want to enter BT with maximum advantage for guaranteed pressure.

The +5 BT advantage from 1P+K means your BT 4K is effectively i10, which is faster than any normal in the game. After a successful 1P+K duck, the opponent literally cannot press a button without eating a launch. This makes 1P+K → BT 4K one of the most oppressive sequences in DOA6 — and the reason why experienced players will start throwing or using tracking mids whenever they see Ayane crouching at close range.

214P: The Offensive Spin (Hajin-Ryu)

Input: 214P (quarter-circle back + P). Startup: i22. Transition to BT: +2 on block, full combo on hit.

214P is fundamentally different from 4P and 1P+K — it is not an evasion tool, it is an offensive approach tool. At i22, it is far too slow to use on reaction to anything. Instead, 214P serves as a gap-closer that transitions Ayane into BT at advantageous range. Use 214P when:

  • You are at mid-range and want to close distance while entering BT safely.
  • The opponent is walking backward or playing passively — 214P punishes passive play by putting you directly in their face in BT stance.
  • You have conditioned the opponent to expect a 3K or 6P at mid-range and want to change the pace.

At +2 on block, 214P gives you BT advantage but not guaranteed pressure — the opponent can contest BT 4K (effectively i13 at +2) with a fast jab. However, the threat is enough: if the opponent presses a button and you use BT 6P instead (effectively i11), you will counter-hit them. If they block, BT T will catch them if they are not throw-breaking. 214P is about forcing the opponent into your BT guessing game from a position where they thought they were safe.

Back-Turned (BT) stance is where Ayane transforms from an evasive poke character into one of the most dangerous offensive threats in DOA6. When Ayane is BT, her entire move list changes — she has a completely different set of normals, strings, launchers, throws, and special moves that are only accessible from the BT position. Mastering BT stance means learning an entirely separate character within the character, and it is the single most important skill for any Ayane player.

The Four-Layer BT Mix-Up

Ayane's BT offense operates in four layers. The layers are ordered by how you should introduce them to an opponent over the course of a set:

Layer 1 — The Strike/Throw Baseline (BT 4K / BT T): The most fundamental BT mix-up. BT 4K is a mid launcher — if the opponent blocks standing, they are safe from the launcher but vulnerable to BT T (command throw). If they crouch to avoid the throw, BT 4K launches them. This is a true 50/50 — the opponent must guess between standing block (beats BT 4K, loses to BT T) and crouching (beats BT T, loses to BT 4K). At F-Rank to C-Rank, this single layer will win you matches.

Layer 2 — The Low Introduction (BT 2K): When opponents start fuzzy-guarding (rapidly alternating between standing and crouching to cover both options), introduce BT 2K. The low kick does less damage than BT 4K but is safer and hits opponents who are standing. After 2-3 BT 2K hits, the opponent starts crouching in BT — at which point BT 4K is guaranteed. The low layer also serves as a conditioning tool: every BT 2K you land makes the opponent more likely to crouch on the next BT entry, opening them up to the mid launcher.

Layer 3 — The Delayed Layer (BT 6P / BT 3K): When opponents start pressing buttons to interrupt your BT offense (which is the correct counter-play), use BT 6P. At i13 and +1 on block, BT 6P stuffs their interrupt attempt and leaves you plus — you can then continue pressure with BT 4K, BT T, or another BT 6P. If opponents start sidestepping your BT pressure, BT 3K (tracking) catches them. This layer is about punishing their adaptation — every time they adjust, you have a BT tool that beats their adjustment.

Layer 4 — The Armored Read (BT 4H+K): When opponents become hyper-aggressive about interrupting your BT entries, use BT 4H+K. This is Ayane's Break Blow from BT — it has armor starting at frame 5 and launches on hit. If the opponent is mashing fast buttons the moment you enter BT, BT 4H+K will armor through their button and launch them for a full combo. This is a hard read tool (uses your Break Gauge) but it makes opponents think twice about overly aggressive BT interruption, which makes all your other BT options safer.

THE BT PRESSURE RULE: In any given match, cycle through all four layers at least once. If you only use Layer 1 (BT 4K / BT T), the opponent will adapt by fuzzy-guarding. If you only use Layers 1 and 2, they will start interrupting. If you never use Layer 4, they will interrupt with impunity. The opponent should never feel comfortable against your BT pressure — and comfort comes from knowing which option is coming. Remove their comfort by rotating layers.

Optimal Juggle Routes: BnB, Wall Carry, Break Blow & Counter Hit

Ayane's combo game revolves around her BT launchers — primarily BT 4K — with optimized routes for every situation. Unlike characters with one-size-fits-all combos, Ayane's juggle enders are height-dependent: the higher the launch, the more hits you can fit before the ender. This means you need different routes for different launcher heights and counter-hit states.

Universal BnB (BT 4K, Normal Hit, Midscreen)

Route: BT 4K → 6KP → BT 4PK → BT 2P+K

Damage: 72

This is your go-to juggle from any BT 4K launcher at standard height. The core juggle sequence is 6KP (two-hit air combo filler) into BT 4PK (re-stand setup) into BT 2P+K (ender). The BT 2P+K ender is the standard Ayane juggle finisher on light and medium-weight characters — it gives excellent knockdown advantage for okizeme. Practice this route until you can execute it cleanly on both sides — it is the foundation of Ayane's entire combo game.

Counter Hit / High Launch Extension

Route: BT 4K (Counter Hit) → 4K → 6K → P+K → BT 4PK → BT 2P+K

Damage: 88 (CH) / 102 (HCH)

On Counter Hit, BT 4K launches significantly higher. The extended route adds a 4K re-launch, 6K juggle filler, and P+K transition before the standard ender. On Hi-Counter Hit (punishing a whiffed hold), the damage reaches 102 — roughly 35% of a standard life bar from a single launcher. This is the route you use when you score a counter-hit confirm from BT, and it is the reason why opponents fear Ayane's BT 4K so much.

Frontal Launcher Combo (8K / 6KK CH)

Route: 8K → 6K → P+K → BT 4PK → BT 2P+K

Damage: 68

For frontal launchers (8K for whiff punishment, 6KK on counter-hit), the route is similar but starts without the BT advantage. The 6K → P+K transition puts Ayane into BT mid-juggle, allowing you to finish with the standard BT ender sequence. This route is slightly lower damage than the BT-launched version but is essential for punishing whiffs and converting stray hits.

Optimal Wall Carry

Route: BT 4K → 66 → PP6PK

Damage: 68 (carries ~70% of stage length)

When you are mid-stage and want to push the opponent to the wall, replace the standard 6KP juggle filler with a dash-cancel (66) into PP6PK. The dash-juggle carries significantly farther than the standard route and the PP6PK ender wall-splats reliably. On smaller stages (The Throwdown, Lost Paradise), this combo will wall-splat from almost any starting position, setting up wall extension combos.

Wall Splat Extension

Route: (Near wall after wall splat) → BT 4H+K → BT 2P+K

Damage: 85 (total with wall splat setup)

After wall-splatting the opponent, quickly re-enter BT (via 4P spin cancel) and use BT 4H+K as a wall-specific ender. The BT 4H+K re-splats the opponent against the wall and BT 2P+K finishes. On stages with breakable walls (Forbidden Fortune, The Muscle), the wall break adds additional unscaled damage, pushing this combo past 100 total damage.

Break Blow Extended Combo

Route: BT 4K → 6KP → Break Blow → BT 4H+K (Wall Splat) → BT 2P+K

Damage: 108 (midscreen), 122 (near wall)

This is your maximum-damage route when you have full Break Gauge. Input the Break Blow immediately after 6KP connects — the Break Blow's knockback carries the opponent to the wall, and BT 4H+K converts the wall splat. At 108-122 damage (roughly 40-45% of a standard life bar), this combo turns a single BT 4K launch into a round-defining moment. Use it when the opponent is at 40% health or less to close out the round.

Heavy-Weight Character Adjustment

On heavy characters (Bass, Bayman, Raidou, Leon), the standard BT 2P+K ender can whiff due to the opponent's larger hurtbox falling faster. In these matchups, substitute BT 2P+K with BT 6P+K as your ender. BT 6P+K has a slightly different trajectory that connects reliably on heavy characters. The damage difference is minimal (2-4 points), so this substitution should be automatic once you have identified a heavy-weight opponent.

Neutral & Spacing: Pokes, Whiff Baiting & The Mid-Range Game

Ayane's neutral game is defined by mid-range dominance through long-reaching pokes and whiff-baiting with spin evasion. She does not want to be full-screen (she lacks projectiles or zoning tools) and she does not want to be point-blank (her fastest buttons lose to dedicated rushdown characters). Ayane's optimal range is roughly 2-3 character widths — close enough that her 3K and 6P connect, far enough that she can react to approaches with 4P spin.

The Mid-Range Toolkit

  • 3K (i14, -5 on block): Your primary footsie tool. At -5, 3K is technically unsafe on block at very close range, but at Ayane's preferred mid-range spacing, the pushback makes it safe against all but the longest-range punishes. The +2 on hit gives you a slight frame advantage to either continue with 6P pressure or back-step into spin evasion. Throw out 3K whenever the opponent is walking forward or trying to establish their own mid-range presence.
  • 6P (i12, +0 on block): Your fastest mid and a decent poke at mid-range thanks to its forward movement. At +0 on block, it resets the situation to neutral, which is fine — Ayane does not need plus frames to succeed, she needs the opponent to press buttons so she can spin. Use 6P as a check — if the opponent blocks it and presses their own button, you now have the read to 4P spin and punish.
  • 2P (i13, -2 on block): Your low poke. Use 2P to interrupt high-heavy pressure and to layer the high/low dimension into your mid-range poking. After 2-3 blocked 3Ks, the opponent is conditioned to stand-block at mid-range — a single 2P will catch them off guard and give you the +1 advantage to start your offense.

The Whiff-Bait Loop

Ayane's neutral game operates on a simple but devastating loop:

  1. Poke with 3K: Throw out 3K at max range. If it hits, you are at +2 and can continue pressure or enter BT via 214P. If it is blocked, you are safe at range.
  2. Micro-backdash: After a blocked 3K, micro-backdash (44 input for 2-3 frames, then block). This creates just enough space that the opponent's longest-range button will whiff if they try to punish your 3K.
  3. Observe the response: Most opponents will try to take their turn after blocking 3K. If they whiff a button due to your backdash, punish with 8K → full combo. If they dash forward to close the gap, meet them with 4P spin → BT 4K. If they do nothing, repeat step 1.

This loop — poke, create space, observe, punish — is the heart of Ayane's footsie game. It requires patience and the discipline to not over-commit, but it makes Ayane one of the most frustrating characters to approach in DOA6 because every engagement starts on her terms.

THE AYANE FOOTSIE RULE: Never commit to a second button after a blocked 3K or 6P unless you have a specific read. Against aggressive opponents, backdash after every blocked poke — they will whiff, and you will punish. Against passive opponents, use 214P after every blocked poke — they are waiting, and 214P puts you directly in their face in BT stance for free. Your neutral game is entirely reactive, and that is its strength.

Okizeme & Setplay: Post-Knockdown Pressure Sequences

Ayane's okizeme (wake-up pressure) is among the strongest in DOA6 because her BT stance allows her to threaten high-damage launchers, throws, lows, and armored options from the same position. After any knockdown that leaves Ayane in BT stance (which is most of her combo enders), the opponent faces a layered guessing game before they can even stand up.

Core Okizeme Setup: BT 2P+K Ender → BT Oki

After ending a combo with BT 2P+K, Ayane remains in BT stance at roughly +15 advantage over the opponent's wake-up. From this position, you have five primary options:

The okizeme game is about pattern recognition: On the first knockdown, use BT 4K to see what the opponent does. If they block standing, next knockdown use BT T. If they start wake-up kicking, next knockdown use BT 4H+K to armor through. If they start wake-up throwing, backdash (BT 44) to make their throw whiff and punish. Every response the opponent has to your oki has a counter — your job is to identify which response they favor and exploit it.

The Oki Conditioning Sequence

Here is a four-knockdown conditioning sequence that works against most intermediate opponents:

  1. First knockdown: BT 4K immediately. Most opponents will eat it or block standing. Observe their response.
  2. Second knockdown (if they blocked): BT T immediately. They blocked the first time, so they are conditioned to block — the throw punishes this. If they blocked again, BT T lands.
  3. Third knockdown (if they wake-up kicked): BT 4H+K. They woke up kicking because they don't want to block or eat throws — the Break Blow armors through their kick and launches them.
  4. Fourth knockdown (if they started waking up throwing): Slight delay → BT 4K. The delay avoids their throw, and BT 4K launches them in recovery.
THE OKIZEME RULE: Every knockdown is an experiment. Do not use the same oki option twice in a row unless the opponent has proven they cannot adapt (some players never learn to stop blocking, in which case BT T them to death). The goal of Ayane oki is not just the damage from each individual guess — it is the mental fatigue of being forced to guess correctly every single time you get knocked down. Over a full set, the opponent will guess wrong 2-3 times, and 2-3 wrong guesses against Ayane oki is enough damage to lose the match.

Matchup Strategy: How to Beat Every Archetype

Ayane's approach changes based on the opponent's character archetype. Here is the high-level game plan against each of the four major DOA6 archetypes.

vs. Rushdown (Kasumi, Christie, Hitomi, Jann Lee)

Rushdown characters want to be in your face pressing plus-on-block buttons. Ayane's counter-strategy relies on 1P+K high-crush and 4P spin evasion to escape their pressure and reverse the momentum. Rushdown characters rely heavily on high jabs (Kasumi's 6P, Christie's 3P, Hitomi's 6P) — 1P+K goes under all of them at frame 1 and transitions you to BT at +5. Once you have ducked a jab and are in BT at +5, the rushdown character is now the one forced to guess. Your goal against rushdown is to convert every blocked string into a spin-read situation: if they continue pressure with a high, 1P+K punishes. If they continue with a mid, 4P evades and punishes. If they pause expecting a spin, take your turn with 6P pressure. Never let a rushdown character complete two full strings without spinning at least once — the threat of your spin is what keeps them from going fully unga-bunga.

vs. Grapplers (Bass, Tina, Bayman, Leon)

Grapplers want you to block so they can start their command throw vortex. Against grapplers, Ayane's game plan is play at mid-range and never block more than one hit. Grappler offense relies on you freezing up. Ayane's 3K at max range completely out-ranges every grappler's fastest button, and 4P spin evades their slow, powerful approach tools (Bass's 6H+K, Tina's 66K, Bayman's 3K). The one specific caution against grapplers: do not enter BT stance at close range against them. Grapplers have the highest throw damage in the game, and BT Ayane cannot break throws. If a Bass player reads your BT transition and throws you, you are eating 80+ damage. Against grapplers, use BT only after hard knockdowns (where they cannot throw you during transition) or from 214P at mid-range (where they cannot reach you during transition). Your frontal poking game is strong enough to beat grapplers — you do not need to force BT to win this matchup.

vs. Setplay/Zoners (Hayabusa, Nyotengu, Phase 4, Marie Rose)

Setplay characters create space with evasive movement and then force you to navigate trap setups. Against these characters, Ayane's 214P Hajin-Ryu spin is her best tool — it covers distance quickly and transitions directly into BT stance at close range, bypassing their space-control game entirely. When Hayabusa uses Ongyoin stance to create space, 214P closes the gap instantly. When Nyotengu takes to the air, 214P puts you underneath her with BT pressure ready. The key against setplay is patience: they want you to chase them and make mistakes. Use 3K and 214P to advance safely, and never dash forward blindly — a patient Ayane who advances with 214P beats an impatient one who dashes into traps every time.

vs. Hybrid/All-Rounders (Rig, Diego, Kokoro, Brad Wong)

Hybrid characters are the most straightforward matchup for Ayane — they play a fundamentally honest game without overwhelming speed, range, or mix-up depth. Against hybrids, Ayane should play her standard layered offense: mid-range 3K poking into 214P BT entries, BT 4K/BT T mix-ups, and okizeme pressure on every knockdown. Hybrids lack the specific tools to counter any layer of Ayane's offense, which means they must guess correctly on every layer — and guessing correctly 3-4 times in a row is statistically unlikely. The only caution against hybrids is to respect their damage output (Diego and Rig can hit hard on counter-hit) and not to force BT entries when you are at a life deficit — play safe, poke them down, and let your superior mix-up depth create openings naturally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is Ayane's playstyle in DOA6 Last Round?
Ayane is a setplay-evasion hybrid who excels at controlling space through spinning stance movements and back-turned stance mix-ups. Her game plan revolves around making the opponent whiff with her unique spin evasion (4P or 1P+K), then punishing with back-turned launchers that lead into high-damage juggle routes. Unlike rushdown characters who want to stay in your face, Ayane thrives at mid-range where her spinning tools and long-reaching pokes can bait reactions. Once she scores a knockdown, her okizeme pressure from back-turned stance (BT) is among the most oppressive in the game, forcing opponents into layered guessing situations on wake-up.
Q: Is Ayane good for beginners in DOA6?
Ayane is a moderate-to-high difficulty character. She is more accessible than Hayabusa but more demanding than Hitomi or Kasumi. Her core challenges are: (1) mastering back-turned stance — roughly 40% of her offense originates from BT, which requires you to learn a separate set of moves and combos, (2) controlling spinning stance momentum — her spin moves create space but also shift your position, requiring strong spatial awareness, and (3) optimizing juggle routes — Ayane's juggles are height-dependent, meaning you need different combo routes for different launcher heights and counter-hit states. Beginners can pick up Ayane and have fun with her basic 4P spin evasion and BT 4K launcher, but reaching her competitive ceiling requires 30-50 hours of dedicated practice, particularly on back-turned transitions and okizeme setups.
Q: What are Ayane's best moves in DOA6?
Ayane's core toolkit revolves around ten essential moves: 4P (spinning stance entry — evades linear attacks and transitions to back-turned at +3), 1P+K (low-profile spin that goes under highs and transitions to BT at +5), BT 4K (i15 back-turned launcher — your primary combo starter), BT 6P (i13 back-turned mid, +1 on block — fundamental BT pressure tool), 6P (i12 mid, +0 on block — main frontal poke), 3K (i14 mid kick, +2 on hit — excellent neutral tool), 2P (i13 low — fastest low poke for interrupting pressure), 214P (Hajin-Ryu spinning punch — long-range approach tool that transitions to BT), BT T (back-turned command throw — 55 damage, beats blocking opponents), and BT 4H+K (armored Break Blow shortcut from BT for clutch situations).
Q: How do I use Ayane's spinning stance effectively?
Ayane's spinning stance (accessed via 4P, 1P+K, or 214P) is her defining mechanic. 4P is your primary spin: it has high evasion frames starting at frame 4, making it one of the best anti-linear tools in DOA6. Use 4P to evade predictable jabs and mids, then punish with BT 4K launcher. 1P+K is the low-profile spin: it goes under highs starting at frame 1 and transitions to BT at +5, making it excellent against high-heavy pressure strings. 214P is the offensive spin: it covers substantial distance and transitions to BT at +2 on block. The golden rule is to never spin predictably — if you spin every time you are at slight disadvantage, opponents will start using tracking moves or delaying buttons to catch your spin recovery. Layer spins with regular blocking, sidesteps, and occasional 2P interrupts to keep the opponent honest.
Q: What are Ayane's best combo routes in DOA6?
Ayane's combo game centers on three launchers: BT 4K (i15, from back-turned — most reliable combo starter), 6KK (two-hit string, second kick launches on counter-hit), and 8K (i14 standing launcher for whiff punishment). The universal BnB is: BT 4K → 6KP → BT 4PK → BT 2P+K for 72 damage. For frontal launchers, use 8K → 6K → P+K → BT 4PK → BT 2P+K for 68 damage. For wall carry, substitute with BT 4K → 66 → PP6PK. For max damage with Break Blow: BT 4K → 6KP → Break Blow → BT 4H+K → BT 2P+K for 108-122 damage. On heavy-weight characters, substitute the BT 2P+K ender with BT 6P+K. Counter-hit confirms extend with an additional 4K → 6K → P+K sequence for 88-102 damage.
Q: How do I counter Ayane in DOA6?
Countering Ayane requires denying her space and exploiting her defensive gaps. Her primary weakness is tracking: most of her key neutral tools (3K, 6P, 4P spin) are linear — sidestep beats a large portion of her frontal offense. Her second weakness is BT transition vulnerability: when Ayane spins into BT stance, she has roughly 8-10 frames of transition where she cannot block — if you predict the spin, a fast mid poke during the transition window will counter-hit her. Her third and most exploitable weakness is BT throw vulnerability: back-turned Ayane cannot throw-break — any throw attempt on a BT Ayane is guaranteed. This is the single most effective counter to her BT pressure: if Ayane enters BT at close range, throw her immediately. The best characters against Ayane are those with fast, tracking mids (Hitomi, Jann Lee) and strong throw games (Bass, Tina, Bayman).
