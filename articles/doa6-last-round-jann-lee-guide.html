DOA6 Last Round Jann Lee Guide: Dragon Gunner, Combos, Frame Data & Complete Strategy

Jann Lee — the self-proclaimed "strongest fighter in the world" — is DOA6 Last Round's answer to Bruce Lee, blending lightning-fast Jeet Kune Do-inspired strikes with the powerful Dragon Gunner stance. If you want a character who controls the pace of the match with dominant mid-range pokes, converts stray hits into devastating stance pressure, and punishes mistakes with explosive Dragon Falls throws, Jann Lee is your fighter. This comprehensive guide covers every tool in his arsenal, from his foundational poke game to his advanced Dragon Gunner mind games and optimal combo routes.

Character Overview

Jann Lee is a mid-range rushdown character with exceptional poke-based neutral, strong stance pressure through Dragon Gunner, and some of the best wall damage in DOA6 Last Round. He excels at controlling the pace of the match with fast, safe mid-range tools, then converting any hit into sustained Dragon Gunner offense that forces the opponent into a high-risk guessing game.

His playstyle revolves around three pillars: dominant neutral poking (6P, 7K, 66K control mid-range beautifully), Dragon Gunner stance pressure (converting pokes into stance mix-ups for massive damage), and Dragon Falls command throw (punishing overly defensive opponents for 65 damage). Jann Lee rewards players who understand spacing, frame advantage, and conditioning — he is a character who grows with your fighting game knowledge.

Key Moves & Essential Tools

Jann Lee's move list is extensive, but a core set of approximately 12 moves forms the foundation of his gameplan. Master these before exploring the rest of his toolkit.

Primary Pokes

  • 6P (i12, +2 on block) — Jann Lee's fastest mid poke. At +2 on block, it sets up frame traps and Dragon Gunner entry. This is the move you will use more than any other. The +2 advantage means the opponent must respect your follow-up or risk getting counter-hit.
  • 7K (i14, -3 on block) — A fast mid kick with excellent range and pushback. Safe on block, great for whiff punishment, and controls spacing at mid-range. Use this when 6P cannot reach.
  • 66K (i15, -5 on block) — A forward-lunging mid kick with superb range that wall splats on hit. Slightly minus on block but the pushback makes it difficult to punish. Essential for wall carry combos and catching opponents at the tip of mid-range.

Close-Range Tools

  • P (i10) — Jann Lee's fastest button. Use it to interrupt pressure, check the opponent, and start short strings.
  • PP (i10, string) — Two-hit punch string. The second hit is delayable, which makes it a useful frame trap tool.
  • 6K (i14) — A close-range mid kick that combos into his BnB routes on hit. Fast enough to use after +frame situations.
  • 41236T (Dragon Falls — 65 damage) — Jann Lee's command throw. Slower than a regular throw but does significantly more damage and has a unique animation that looks intimidating. Use this to punish opponents who block too much after Dragon Gunner pressure.

Low Attacks

  • 6H+K (i20) — A long-range low kick that crushes highs. Slow but covers excellent distance and knocks down on hit. Use it to catch opponents who are standing at mid-range.
  • 2K (i14) — A quick low kick from crouch. Fast enough to use in close-range mix-ups and plus enough on hit to continue pressure.
  • Dragon Gunner 2K — A low sweep from Dragon Gunner stance that causes a knockdown. This is the primary low option in Dragon Gunner mix-ups and leads to okizeme pressure.

Special Tools

  • P+K (i17) — A powerful mid punch that causes Critical State on hit, guaranteeing a follow-up combo. Slow but rewarding — use it to launch opponents who are not respecting your poke pressure.
  • 4H+K (Deja Vu kick, i18) — The signature move covered in depth below. A mid kick with a backstep evasion property that wall splats on hit.
  • 214P — Dragon Gunner stance entry. The gateway to Jann Lee's most damaging offense. Covered in depth in the next section.
FRAME TRAP BASICS: After 6P on block (+2), your 6K (i14) will trade with the opponent's fastest mid (i12). Your 7K (i14) will beat anything slower. If the opponent respects your plus frames and blocks, use 214P to enter Dragon Gunner for stance pressure. This is the core of Jann Lee's offense.

Dragon Gunner Stance Deep Dive

Dragon Gunner (214P) is Jann Lee's signature stance and the key to his highest-damage offense. When Jann Lee enters this stance, he assumes a distinctive forward-leaning posture with his lead hand extended. The stance transforms his neutral game into a close-range guessing game that the opponent must navigate correctly or eat massive damage.

How to Use Dragon Gunner Effectively

The power of Dragon Gunner comes from the Triangle System applied to stance. The opponent must choose between blocking (vulnerable to Dragon Falls throw — 65 damage), standing (vulnerable to Dragon Sweep low — 18 damage + okizeme), or crouching (vulnerable to Dragon Upper launcher — 20 damage + full combo). Each correct read by Jann Lee yields significant damage, and each incorrect guess by the opponent resets the situation with continued pressure.

Entry Points into Dragon Gunner:

  • 214P — Direct entry from neutral. Use after +frame moves like 6P (+2 on block) to enter safely.
  • 4H+K~214P — Deja Vu kick cancelled into Dragon Gunner. The backstep evades the opponent's retaliation while transitioning into stance. This is the most advanced and rewarding entry method.
  • After wall splat — Certain wall combo routes leave you at +frames where Dragon Gunner entry is free.
  • After knockdown — Enter Dragon Gunner as the opponent wakes up for immediate okizeme pressure.

Conditioning Pattern: Start by showing Dragon Sweep (2K) two or three times to make the opponent crouch on wake-up. Once they start blocking low, switch to Dragon Upper (P) for a full launcher combo. When they freeze up from fear of both options, introduce Dragon Falls (T) command throw to punish their passivity. The key is to never become predictable — rotate all three options and watch the opponent's defensive habits carefully.

ADVANCED TIP — STANCE CANCEL MIND GAMES: Use Dragon Gunner cancel (66) to feint a stance approach. Enter Dragon Gunner at mid-range, cancel immediately with 66, and the opponent will often whiff a panic button trying to interrupt your nonexistent stance follow-up. Punish the whiff with 66K or P+K for a free launch. This technique elevates Jann Lee from a stance character to a true mind game character.

Deja Vu Kick Mastery

The Deja Vu kick (4H+K) is Jann Lee's signature special move and one of the most unique tools in DOA6 Last Round. It is a powerful mid kick with a built-in backstep evasion property — during the startup frames, Jann Lee leans backward, evading high attacks and some mid-range mids. On hit, it causes a spinning stun that guarantees a follow-up combo. On block, it is safe with pushback. And when cancelled into Dragon Gunner (4H+K~214P), it becomes a stance transition that evades and attacks simultaneously.

When to Use Deja Vu Kick

  • Whiff Punishment: At mid-range, if the opponent whiffs a slow move, 4H+K covers distance and evades potential counter-hits. The backstep makes it safer than a raw 66K approach.
  • Counter-Hit Fishing: After your +2 frame trap from 6P, the opponent may try to press a button. Deja Vu kick's backstep evades their high retaliation and counter-hits their mid attempt.
  • Wall Splat Tool: Deja Vu kick wall splats on hit, making it an essential wall combo component. Near a wall, confirm into wall splat into optimal wall combo for massive damage.
  • Dragon Gunner Transition: The cancel into Dragon Gunner (4H+K~214P) is Jann Lee's most advanced technique. The backstep evades, the stance entry threatens, and the opponent is forced into an immediate guessing game.
EXECUTION TIP: The 4H+K~214P cancel requires buffering the 214P input during the Deja Vu kick animation. Practice this in Training Mode with the input display on — the 214P must be input during the backstep frames, not after the kick has already landed. Start by practicing the cancel on its own, then practice confirming the cancel only when Deja Vu is blocked (the hit confirm window is generous).

Wall Carry & Stage Damage

Jann Lee has excellent wall carry thanks to 66K's forward momentum and PPP's multi-hit extension. His wall damage is among the best in the game due to Deja Vu kick's wall splat property.

Wall Carry Route

Launcher → 66K → PPP → 6K → PP6PK — This route carries approximately half the stage and works consistently from both sides. The PPP extension adds distance while 6K relaunches near the wall for the final ender.

Wall Combo (After Wall Splat)

Wall Splat → 4H+K → 6K → PP6PK — After any wall splat (from 66K, 4H+K, or 6P+K near wall), use Deja Vu kick to relaunch, then finish with the standard BnB ender. This does approximately 70–80 damage depending on the wall splat starter.

Optimal Wall Combo with Break Blow

Wall Splat → Break Blow → 4H+K → 6K → PP6PK — If you have Break Gauge available, the Break Blow extension after wall splat adds 15–20 damage to the wall combo. This is Jann Lee's highest damage route and can deal 90+ damage in optimal conditions.

Okizeme & Wake-Up Pressure

Jann Lee's okizeme game is deceptively strong. After any knockdown — whether from Dragon Sweep (DG 2K), throw, or combo ender — Jann Lee has multiple options to pressure the opponent on wake-up.

Primary Okizeme Options

  • Dragon Gunner Entry on Wake-Up: After a knockdown, immediately enter Dragon Gunner (214P) as the opponent stands up. They must immediately choose between blocking (vulnerable to Dragon Falls 65-damage throw), standing (vulnerable to Dragon Sweep low), or crouching (vulnerable to Dragon Upper launcher). This is your strongest okizeme option.
  • Meaty 6P: Time a 6P to hit on the first active frame of the opponent's wake-up. If it counter-hits, you get +5 advantage and a free combo follow-up. If they block, you are still +2 for continued pressure.
  • Throw: If the opponent is conditioned to block on wake-up, a regular throw or Dragon Falls command throw (41236T) punishes their passivity for 65 damage.
  • 6H+K (Low): A long-range low kick that catches opponents who stand up and try to walk backward. Knockdown on hit leads to another okizeme situation.

Matchup Strategies

vs. Grapplers (Tina, Bass, Bayman)

Jann Lee's poke game dominates grapplers at mid-range. Keep them out with 6P and 7K, and only enter Dragon Gunner when you have frame advantage. Their command throws are slower than your Dragon Falls, so in the throw exchange, Jann Lee often wins. Key: Do not let grapplers get close enough to start their throw game — use 66K to wall splat them before they can establish their offense.

vs. Ninjas (Kasumi, Hayabusa, Ayane)

Ninja characters are fast and evasive, which challenges Jann Lee's linear poke game. Use 6P to stuff their teleport and stance transitions, and rely on Deja Vu kick's backstep to evade their rush-down strings. Key: Dragon Gunner P (launcher) beats most ninja stance options on reaction — if you see them enter a stance, immediately enter Dragon Gunner and launch.

vs. Heavy Characters (Leon, Raidou)

Heavy characters have more health but are slower, which gives Jann Lee time to establish his mid-range dominance. Use 66K aggressively to carry them to the wall, then unleash Jann Lee's devastating wall combos. Key: Heavy characters struggle against Dragon Gunner mix-ups because their slower buttons cannot interrupt your stance pressure.

vs. Zoners (Helena, Kokoro)

Zoning characters want to keep Jann Lee at their preferred range with long pokes. Use sidestep to beat their linear tools, then close the gap with 66K or Dragon Gunner cancel (66) into dash. Key: Patience is essential — do not rush in recklessly. Wait for their poke to whiff, then punish with 66K or P+K.

Training Routine

Phase 1: Fundamentals (Week 1–2)

Goals: Consistent 6P/7K/66K poke game. Basic BnB combos from every launcher (8K, DG P, P+K, CH 4H+K). Throw break practice against all throw types.

Practice Routine: 15 minutes of poke spacing drills (set dummy to walk back and forth, practice hitting 6P at max range), 15 minutes of BnB combo practice from each launcher, 10 minutes of throw break drills. Total daily investment: 40 minutes.

Rank Expectation: D to C-Rank. At this stage you are winning with Jann Lee's strong normals and simple combos — Dragon Gunner is not yet part of your gameplan, and that is fine.

Phase 2: Stance Integration (Week 3–4)

Goals: Comfortable Dragon Gunner entry from 6P on block. React to Dragon Gunner follow-ups (P, K, 2K, T) based on opponent behavior. Deja Vu kick basic usage (raw 4H+K in neutral).

Practice Routine: 10 minutes of Dragon Gunner follow-up drills (set dummy to random block/crouch, practice choosing the correct follow-up on reaction), 15 minutes of 6P → Dragon Gunner entry practice, 15 minutes of ranked matches focusing only on integrating Dragon Gunner into your existing gameplan. Total daily investment: 40 minutes.

Rank Expectation: C to B-Rank. Dragon Gunner pressure is now part of your offense, and you are starting to convert stray hits into stance mix-ups. Your damage output has increased significantly.

Phase 3: Advanced Play (Week 5+)

Goals: Deja Vu cancel into Dragon Gunner (4H+K~214P) used in real matches. Optimal wall combo routing. Dragon Gunner cancel (66) mind games. Full okizeme game with Dragon Gunner on wake-up.

Practice Routine: This phase is about integration. Your daily routine shifts to 70% ranked matches and 30% labbing — because at this level, the lab work is matchup-specific (labbing the one character you lost to yesterday) rather than general execution drills. After every ranked session, note the one character who gave you the most trouble and spend 15 minutes in Training Mode labbing their pressure strings and finding your punish windows.

Rank Expectation: B+ to S-Rank. A complete Jann Lee at this level controls every range of the match, converts every hit into optimized damage, and forces the opponent into an endless cycle of Dragon Gunner guessing games. His poke game keeps you safe while his stance pressure keeps the opponent scared.

THE JANN LEE MINDSET: Jann Lee rewards discipline and punishes impatience. The players who struggle with Jann Lee are the ones who enter Dragon Gunner without frame advantage, throw out Deja Vu kicks randomly in neutral, or rush in recklessly against characters who excel at close range. The players who succeed with Jann Lee play him like a chess game — every poke is calculated, every Dragon Gunner entry is set up by frame advantage, and every Deja Vu kick is a response to the opponent's demonstrated tendencies. Control the mid-range first. Then convert. That is Jann Lee.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is Jann Lee's Dragon Gunner stance in DOA6?
Dragon Gunner is Jann Lee's signature stance, entered by pressing 214P or from specific string transitions. In this stance, Jann Lee can perform several follow-ups: P (fast mid punch launcher), K (overhead kick that causes bound), 2K (low sweep for high/low mix-ups), T (command throw for 65 damage), P+K (Break Blow shortcut), and a stance cancel with 66. Dragon Gunner is the core of Jann Lee's offense — it gives him access to his most damaging combo starters and forces the opponent into a guessing game between mid launcher, low sweep, and command throw. The stance is best used after +frame moves like 6P (+2 on block) or after conditioning the opponent with Jann Lee's strong mid-range pokes.
Q: Is Jann Lee good for beginners in DOA6?
Jann Lee is a medium-difficulty character and is a solid choice for beginners who want to learn proper fighting game fundamentals. His poke game is straightforward — 6P, 66K, and 7K are all fast, safe, and intuitive. His Dragon Gunner stance introduces stance-based offense in a manageable way, and his combo routes are not particularly demanding in terms of execution. Where Jann Lee becomes challenging is at higher levels, where maximizing his damage requires confirming Dragon Gunner transitions on reaction and managing his Deja Vu kick mind games under pressure. Beginners can pick up Jann Lee and immediately start winning with his strong normals and simple BnB combos, then gradually integrate Dragon Gunner and advanced okizeme as their understanding deepens.
Q: What are Jann Lee's best moves in DOA6?
Jann Lee's most important tools are: 6P (i12, +2 on block — his fastest mid poke and primary frame trap starter), 66K (i15 mid kick with great range that wall splats), 7K (i14 safe mid kick with good pushback), 214P (Dragon Gunner stance entry), 3K (i14 mid kick that knocks down on Counter Hit), 6H+K (long-range low kick that crushes highs), P+K (powerful mid punch that causes Critical State), and 41236T (Dragon Falls command throw — 65 damage). For defense, his 4H expert hold counters mid kicks with a unique animation. The key to Jann Lee is controlling mid-range with his excellent pokes and then converting stray hits into Dragon Gunner pressure for sustained offense.
Q: How do I use Jann Lee's Deja Vu kick effectively?
The Deja Vu kick (4H+K) is one of Jann Lee's signature moves — a powerful mid kick with a unique backstep animation that evades highs and some mids during its startup. It causes a spinning stun on hit that guarantees a follow-up combo, and on block it is safe with pushback. The Deja Vu kick excels as a whiff punishment tool at mid-range, a CH fishing tool to catch opponents pressing buttons after your plus-frame moves, and a wall splat tool for optimal wall combo damage. Advanced usage involves canceling the Deja Vu backstep into Dragon Gunner (4H+K~214P) to create a stance transition that evades the opponent's retaliation and immediately threatens with Dragon Gunner follow-ups. This cancel is what separates good Jann Lee players from great ones.
Q: What are Jann Lee's best combo routes in DOA6?
Jann Lee's combo game centers around three launchers: Dragon Gunner P (standing launcher from stance, i12 after stance entry), 8K (standing launcher, i13), and P+K (Critical State launcher). The universal BnB is: Launcher → 6K → PP6PK. For wall carry, use Launcher → 66K → PPP → 6K → PP6PK, which carries roughly half the stage. For max damage with Break Blow, the route is: Launcher → 6K → Break Blow → 66K (wall splat) → PP6PK. Near a wall, use Launcher → 66K → 66K (wall splat) → PPK for optimal wall damage. On Counter Hit, confirm Deja Vu kick (4H+K) → 6K → PP6PK. The Dragon Falls command throw (41236T) is not a combo starter but is your primary grounded throw mix-up for 65 damage when opponents are blocking.
Q: How do I counter Jann Lee in DOA6?
Countering Jann Lee requires understanding his three main offensive layers. First, his poke game: 6P and 7K are his primary mid-range tools — sidestep beats both cleanly. Second, his Dragon Gunner stance: the stance has no inherent invincibility, so use a fast mid poke (i11–i13) to interrupt his stance entry or stuff his follow-ups if he enters at close range. Third, his Deja Vu kick (4H+K): the backstep evades highs, so always use a mid or low to challenge it. His key weaknesses are: limited range on his fastest buttons (6P and 7K are both relatively short-range), a reliance on Dragon Gunner for max damage which can be interrupted, and no safe approach tool at full screen. The best strategy against Jann Lee is to fight at long range where his pokes cannot reach, use sidestep to beat his linear mid-range tools, and pressure him aggressively when he enters Dragon Gunner at close range.
