DOA6 Last Round Christie Guide: Combos, Frame Data & Complete Strategy for Mastering the Snake Assassin

Christie is the embodiment of relentless pressure in Dead or Alive 6 Last Round. With her She-Qu snake stance, blistering string speed, and a mix-up game that forces opponents to guess on every interaction, she is one of the most suffocating close-range characters in the entire roster. Her Chinese She-Qu (Snake Fist) style translates into a fast, slippery playstyle that rewards aggressive players who love to stay in their opponent's face. This guide covers every tool in her arsenal: from her fastest pokes and optimal combo routes to She-Qu stance mix-ups, wall carry sequences, Break Blow extensions, and matchup-specific strategies that will turn your Christie from a masher into a calculated predator.

1. Christie Overview: Playstyle, Strengths & Weaknesses

Christie is classified as a rushdown/mix-up character. Unlike Kasumi's teleport-based pressure or Hayabusa's stance-based mind games, Christie's gameplan revolves around her She-Qu stance transitions and string-based pressure that never lets the opponent rest. She excels at smothering opponents with fast, ambiguous mix-ups that require split-second reads to defend against.

Playstyle Summary

  • Archetype: Rushdown / Mix-up
  • Health: 950 HP (below average — dies faster than most characters)
  • Difficulty: Intermediate to Advanced
  • Core Strength: She-Qu stance mix-ups, fast strings, sidestep evasion
  • Core Weakness: Low health, linear neutral, meter dependency for max damage

Strengths

  • Blistering string speed: Her PP strings and 6P are among the fastest in the game, making it difficult for opponents to take their turn back.
  • She-Qu stance terror: The snake stance creates 50/50 mix-ups on every transition — mid, low, throw, or guard break — forcing opponents to guess constantly.
  • Excellent sidestep: Christie has one of the best sidesteps in DOA6, allowing her to evade linear attacks and create whiff-punish opportunities.
  • String pressure: Many of her strings have built-in mix-ups (high-low, mid-low) that are difficult to fuzzy guard consistently.
  • Strong wall carry: PP6PK and her various string enders carry opponents toward walls naturally, leading to high-damage wall combos.

Weaknesses

  • 950 HP: She has one of the lowest health pools in the game. Combined with her need to be at close range, this means mistakes are punished harder than average.
  • Linear neutral game: Her best approach tools (66P, 66K, 236P) are all mid attacks that can be sidestepped by observant opponents.
  • No strong long-range tools: She lacks the projectile or long-range poke tools that characters like Jann Lee or Leifang possess.
  • Meter dependency: Her highest damage combos require Break Blow meter, and without it her damage output is merely average.
  • Predictable She-Qu patterns: If the opponent reads your She-Qu transitions, they can interrupt with fast jabs or holds on reaction.
Pro Tip: Christie rewards players who can maintain constant close-range pressure without becoming predictable. The key is never doing the same She-Qu sequence twice — rotate your options to keep opponents permanently guessing.

3. She-Qu Stance: The Complete Snake Guide

Christie's She-Qu (Snake) stance is the engine of her offense. Understanding how to enter it, what options are available, and when to use each one separates good Christie players from terrifying ones.

How to Enter She-Qu

  • 236 input: Raw entry from neutral. Use this to approach from mid-range.
  • 6P+K: Transitions on hit or block. Safe at -3, so you maintain pressure.
  • 3P+K: Transitions with better tracking. Use against sidestep-happy opponents.
  • Specific string cancels: Some strings have She-Qu cancel points that create ambiguous timing.

She-Qu Options

Input Startup On Block Properties Usage
She-Qu~P 13f -5 Mid punch Fastest She-Qu option. Combos on CH into full combo. Use to check opponents.
She-Qu~K 16f -7 Low kick Unseeable low from certain transitions. Full combo on CH. The mix-up killer.
She-Qu~P+K 20f +3 Guard break mid Your best friend — +3 on block means it's always your turn after. Forces respect.
She-Qu~T 12f N/A Command throw, 50 dmg Punishes opponents who freeze and block after She-Qu entry.
She-Qu~H N/A N/A Hold/cancel Cancel She-Qu to return to neutral. Use to bait whiffed holds.

She-Qu Mix-Up Flowchart

The key to effective She-Qu pressure is understanding the risk-reward of each option:

  • Opponent blocks after 6P+K: Go She-Qu~P+K (guard break, +3) → continue pressure.
  • Opponent tries to jab out: Go She-Qu~P (13f, beats jabs on CH) → full combo.
  • Opponent stands and blocks: Go She-Qu~K (low) → combo on CH.
  • Opponent freezes completely: Go She-Qu~T (command throw, 50 damage).
  • Opponent tries to hold: Cancel with She-Qu~H → punish the hold recovery.
Pro Tip: The most important She-Qu principle is never be predictable. If you always go She-Qu~P+K after 6P+K, opponents will start interrupting with fast attacks. Mix between all five options — the moment your opponent starts guessing, you've already won.

5. Wall Carry & Wall Combo Optimization

Christie's wall carry is one of her strongest attributes. PP6PK has excellent forward momentum that naturally carries opponents toward walls, and her She-Qu relaunches extend carry distance significantly.

Wall Carry Principles

  • PP6PK is your primary carry tool: It has the best forward momentum and wall splat properties.
  • Use 3P+K → She-Qu~P loops: These extend carry distance while building damage.
  • Aim for the wall, not max damage: Sometimes dropping 5 damage to secure a wall splat is worth it for the 30+ extra wall damage.
  • Stage awareness: Know where the walls are before you start your combo. Adjust your route to angle toward the wall.

Optimal Wall Combo Routes

Standard Wall Combo (No Meter)

33P → PP6PK → wall splat → 3P+K → She-Qu~P → PPP — approximately 115-125 damage. This is your bread and butter wall combo.

Break Blow Wall Combo (Full Meter)

33P → Break Blow (hold S, wall splat) → cancel with H → PP6PK → 3P+K → She-Qu~P → PPP — approximately 135-145 damage. The Break Blow wall splat creates a massive extension.

Stage-Specific Notes

  • The Throwdown: Close walls, easy to wall splat from almost any combo route. Christie excels here.
  • Lost Paradise: Walls on three sides. Use side carries (PP6PK angles) to hit walls early.
  • Scramble: Tight corridors with many wall opportunities. Christie's best stage overall.
  • Aerial Gardens: Open center with walls only at edges. Focus carry routes more carefully.

6. Break Blow & Break Hold Christie-Specific Routes

Break Blow Routes

Christie's Break Blow (full meter, S+P+K) is a powerful tool that walls plats on hit and creates massive combo extensions. Here are her optimal Break Blow routes:

Route Damage Notes
33P → Break Blow (hold S) → cancel H → PP6PK → PPP 115-120 Standard meter dump. Reliable and consistent.
33P → Break Blow → cancel H → 3P+K → She-Qu~P → PP6PK 120-125 Optimized route with She-Qu relaunch.
33P → Break Blow → wall splat → cancel H → PP6PK → 3P+K → She-Qu~P → PPP 140-150 Wall combo — maximum damage. Use when near wall.
Break Blow (raw, punish) 65-70 Use as a whiff punish or after a hard read on a slow move.

Break Hold Usage

Christie's Break Hold (hold S during a hold) costs 50% meter and converts a successful hold into a full combo. Use it when:

  • You read a predictable mid kick or high punch — convert the hold into 33P → full combo.
  • Opponent uses the same string repeatedly — Break Hold the predictable hit and launch.
  • You need to make a comeback — the extra damage from a Break Hold combo can swing a round.
Pro Tip: Don't waste meter on Break Hold unless you're confident in the read. Meter is better spent on Break Blow combos for guaranteed damage. Only Break Hold when the opponent's pattern is completely obvious.

7. Okizeme & Wake-Up Pressure

Christie's okizeme (wake-up pressure) is where she truly shines. After knocking opponents down, she can set up terrifying 50/50 situations that are extremely difficult to escape.

Knock-Down Situations

  • After PPP ender: Opponent lands face-down. Walk forward slightly and use 2K (low) or 33P (mid launcher) as they rise.
  • After wall combo: Opponent bounces off wall. Time a 33P to catch all wake-up options except tech-roll.
  • After throw: Standard knockdown. Use 66P to catch back-roll, or walk up and She-Qu mix-up.

Wake-Up Mix-Up Options

  • 2K (low sweep): Catches opponents who stand up blocking high. Fast and safe.
  • 33P (mid launcher): Catches opponents who try to roll or stay on the ground. Launches for full combo.
  • Walk-up She-Qu~T (command throw): Catches opponents who freeze and block on wake-up. 50 damage.
  • Meaty 6P: Time it to hit on the first active frame of wake-up. If it counter-hits, full combo.
  • Nothing (bait): Sometimes the best okizeme is doing nothing — bait the wake-up hold and punish the recovery.

8. Matchup Strategies: Every Archetype Covered

vs. Rushdown (Kasumi, Hayabusa, Jann Lee)

Rushdown mirrors are Christie's most volatile matchups. Both characters want to be at close range, so the player who wins the neutral exchange typically snowballs the round. Key strategies:

  • Win the jab war: Christie's P (10f) and 6P (11f) are your primary tools. After blocking any rushdown string, immediately check with 6P to reclaim your turn.
  • Use sidestep aggressively: Christie's excellent sidestep counters many rushdown approaches. Sidestep into 6P is your bread and butter.
  • Don't be afraid to block: Against heavy rushdown, sometimes the best strategy is to block patiently and wait for a gap. Christie's low health means you can't afford to guess wrong repeatedly.

vs. Grapplers (Bass, Tina, Bayman)

Grapplers are dangerous because their command throws punish Christie's need to block at close range. Key strategies:

  • Never stand still at throw range: Use constant movement — backdash, sidestep, and 2P pokes to stay unpredictable.
  • Favor She-Qu~P over She-Qu~K: The low sweep is more vulnerable to being thrown on reaction. Stick to mid options against grapplers.
  • Punish throw whiffs: When grapplers whiff a command throw, the recovery is long enough for 33P → full combo. Watch for the whiff and punish hard.
  • Break Hold their predictable strings: Grapplers often use the same approach strings. Break Hold the predictable hit and launch for massive damage.

vs. Keepout (Helena, Leifang, Kokoro)

Keepout characters try to keep Christie at mid-range where her tools are weakest. Key strategies:

  • Be patient and close distance slowly: Use 66P and 66K to approach, but don't rush in predictably. Walk forward and block, then explode when in range.
  • Use She-Qu~P+K guard break: Once at close range, She-Qu~P+K (+3 on block) forces the keepout player to respect your offense.
  • Sidestep their pokes: Many keepout tools are linear. Sidestep into 6P or 33P punishes their whiffed pokes.
  • Whiff punish with 236P: She-Qu launcher has deceptive range and punishes keepout tools from just outside their range.

vs. Defensive/Turtle (Hitomi, Eliot)

Defensive characters block well and wait for mistakes. Christie's mix-up game is designed to crack turtles. Key strategies:

  • Use She-Qu~T liberally: Command throws punish passive blocking. Mix She-Qu~T with She-Qu~P+K to create a true 50/50.
  • Vary your string timings: Don't always finish strings. Delay hits, cancel into She-Qu, or stop mid-string to bait holds.
  • Use low pokes to open them up: 2P and 3K chip away at defensive players and force them to block low, opening them up for mid launchers.
  • Okizeme pressure: Defensive players hate being knocked down because Christie's wake-up mix-ups are extremely difficult to deal with.

9. Training Routine: The 20-Minute Daily Drill

Consistent practice is the key to mastering Christie. Here is a focused 20-minute daily routine:

Minutes 1-5: Movement & Sidestep

  • Practice sidestep into 6P — 20 reps on each side.
  • Practice backdash into 66P whiff-punish — 20 reps.
  • Practice forward dash into 33P — 20 reps.

Minutes 5-10: She-Qu Stance

  • Practice all She-Qu entries: 236, 6P+K, 3P+K — 10 reps each.
  • Practice She-Qu option rotation: P → K → P+K → T → cancel — 5 full rotations.
  • Set dummy to random guard and practice reading which She-Qu option to use.

Minutes 10-15: Combos

  • Practice 33P → PP6PK (beginner combo) — 10 reps.
  • Practice 33P → 6P → 3P+K → She-Qu~P → PP6PK (intermediate) — 10 reps.
  • Practice wall combo: 33P → PP6PK → wall splat → 3P+K → She-Qu~P → PPP — 10 reps.

Minutes 15-20: Pressure Sequences

  • Practice 6P+K (block) → She-Qu~P+K (guard break) → 6P (frame trap) — 10 reps.
  • Practice okizeme: PPP knockdown → walk up → 2K/33P mix-up — 10 reps.
  • Practice Break Blow combo route with wall splat — 5 reps.
Pro Tip: Record your Training Mode sessions and watch them back. You'll notice patterns in your own play — always going to the same She-Qu option, always using the same approach tool — that you can fix before they become habits.

10. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Christie good in DOA6 Last Round?
Yes, Christie is a strong A-tier character in DOA6 Last Round. She excels at close-range pressure with her She-Qu snake stance, has some of the fastest strings in the game, excellent sidestep evasion, and a terrifying mix-up game that forces opponents to guess constantly. Her main weakness is below-average health (950 HP) and a somewhat linear neutral game that relies on getting in close. In the hands of an aggressive player who understands frame traps and stance transitions, she is one of the most suffocating rushdown characters in the roster.
What are Christie's best combo starters in DOA6?
Christie's best combo starters are 33P (15-frame mid launcher, her most reliable combo starter from neutral), 236P (18-frame mid launcher from She-Qu stance transition), 9K (16-frame high launcher for anti-air), and 4H+K (19-frame low-crushing mid launcher). For counter-hit fishing, her 6P (11-frame mid) and 3K (14-frame low) are excellent neutral tools that convert into full combos on CH. From She-Qu stance, She-Qu~P (13-frame mid) is a fast confirm into full combos on counter-hit, making stance pressure extremely dangerous.
How do I use Christie's She-Qu stance effectively?
Christie's She-Qu (Snake) stance is entered via 236 input or specific move transitions (6P+K, 3P+K). From She-Qu, she has access to: She-Qu~P (13-frame mid punch, combos on CH), She-Qu~K (16-frame low kick, unseeable from certain transitions), She-Qu~P+K (guard break mid, +3 on block), and She-Qu~T (command throw, 50 damage). The key to effective She-Qu usage is mixing your transitions — enter She-Qu from 6P+K on block to maintain pressure, from 3P+K to catch sidesteps, and from raw 236 to create ambiguous approach angles. Never be predictable with your She-Qu options — rotate between the mid, low, throw, and guard break to keep opponents guessing.
What is Christie's best wall combo route?
Christie's optimal wall combo is: 33P (launcher) → PP6PK (wall carry) → 3P+K (wall splat) → She-Qu~P → PPP (wall ender), dealing approximately 115-125 damage. With Break Blow meter: 33P → Break Blow (hold S, wall splat) → cancel with H → PP6PK → PPP, dealing 135-145 damage. Christie's wall carry is excellent because PP6PK has strong forward momentum and naturally carries toward walls. On stages with close walls like The Throwdown, she can wall splat from almost any launcher with proper positioning.
How do I deal with keepout characters as Christie?
Against keepout characters like Helena and Bayman, Christie must use her movement and stance transitions to close distance safely. Use sidestep 6P (her excellent tracking mid) to beat linear pokes, and close distance with 66P (dashing mid punch) or 66K (dashing mid kick). Once at close range, immediately transition into She-Qu pressure — She-Qu~P+K guard break is +3 on block and forces the opponent to respect your offense. Against heavy keepout, favor 236P (She-Qu launcher) as a whiff-punish tool — its range is deceptive and it leads to high-damage combos. Never rush in predictably — Christie thrives when she patiently waits for the right moment to explode into close-range pressure.
What are Christie's weaknesses in DOA6?
Christie's main weaknesses are: (1) Low health pool of 950 HP — she dies faster than most characters, making every mistake costly; (2) Linear neutral game — her best approach tools (66P, 66K, 236P) are all mid attacks that can be sidestepped, and she lacks long-range pokes; (3) Meter dependency — her highest damage Break Blow routes require full meter, and without meter her damage output is average at best; (4) Predictable high-risk patterns — her She-Qu stance mix-ups are powerful but can be interrupted by fast jabs if the opponent reads the transition. To succeed with Christie, you must master her close-range frame traps and never let opponents breathe at your optimal range.
