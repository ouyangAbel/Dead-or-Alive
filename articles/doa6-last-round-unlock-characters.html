DOA6 Last Round vs Original DOA6: Key Differences

Understanding the unlock situation requires knowing the version history. Dead or Alive 6 launched in March 2019 with 24 base characters. Over the following year, KOEI TECMO released 7 additional fighters through paid DLC and Season Passes. The Last Round edition — released across platforms including Nintendo Switch 2 in 2026 — is the definitive, complete version that bundles everything into one package.

KEY TAKEAWAY: DOA6 Last Round is the "Game of the Year" style complete edition. Every single character — all 31 fighters — is available from the moment you launch the game. No grinding, no Story Mode completion, no DLC purchases required.

Are All Characters Unlocked in DOA6 Last Round?

Yes — every single one of the 31 fighters is fully unlocked from the start in DOA6 Last Round. When you first boot up the game and enter Character Select, you will see the complete grid with no grayed-out slots, no lock icons, and no "purchase required" prompts. This applies to all platforms: PS4, PS5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X|S, PC (Steam), and Nintendo Switch 2.

This is a significant quality-of-life improvement over the original DOA6, where several characters required either Story Mode completion or separate DLC purchases. The Last Round philosophy is simple: buy the game, get every fighter.

PRO TIP: Even though all characters are unlocked, we recommend spending time in Training Mode with a small handful of fighters before jumping into online matches. Having access to 31 characters can be overwhelming — pick 2-3 that match your preferred playstyle and master them first. The 24 Base Roster Characters (Always Unlocked)

The core DOA6 roster features 24 fighters spanning multiple fighting disciplines — from traditional Japanese martial arts to professional wrestling and street fighting. These characters have been available from day one in every version of the game and require no unlocking whatsoever.

Original DOA Veterans (Returning Since DOA1)

The original eight fighters who defined the series:

  • Kasumi — Mugen Tenshin Ninjutsu. The series protagonist, a fast rushdown character with excellent mix-up potential.
  • Ryu Hayabusa — Hayabusa-style Ninjutsu. The legendary ninja with a high-damage, high-execution toolkit including the iconic Izuna Drop.
  • Hayate — Mugen Tenshin Ninjutsu. Kasumi's brother, a balanced all-rounder with strong mid-range pokes and solid hold damage.
  • Jann Lee — Jeet Kune Do. The Dragon Kick master, excels at whiff punishment and frame traps with lightning-fast strikes.
  • Leifang — Tai Chi Quan. A defensive specialist whose advanced holds and counters can shut down aggressive opponents.
  • Tina Armstrong — Professional Wrestling. A grappler with surprisingly fast strikes and devastating chain throws.
  • Bass Armstrong — Professional Wrestling. Tina's father, the slowest but hardest-hitting grappler in the game with massive throw damage.
  • Zack — Muay Thai (self-taught). An unpredictable rushdown fighter with wild animations and tricky mix-ups.

DOA1-DOA3 Era Additions

  • Ayane — Mugen Tenshin Hajin Mon Ninjutsu. Kasumi's half-sister, an evasive fighter with spinning attacks and a unique back-turned stance.
  • Bayman — Commando Sambo. A heavy defensive grappler with advanced hold techniques and high counter-damage potential.
  • Leon — Commando Sambo. Bayman's rival, a more explosive grappler focused on brutal throw chains and ground-and-pound.
  • Helena Douglas — Pi Gua Quan. A stance-heavy character with elegant low stances, crushes highs, and confusing mix-ups.
  • Brad Wong — Zui Quan (Drunken Fist). A tricky stance character whose drunken movements create unpredictable attack angles.
  • Christie — She Quan (Snake Fist). A fast, slippery striker with excellent sidestep attacks and a deadly jab string.
  • Hitomi — Karate. The most beginner-friendly character with simple, effective strings and reliable punishment tools.

DOA4-DOA6 Newcomers

  • Kokoro — Ba Ji Quan. A close-range powerhouse with devastating palm strikes and excellent throw mix-ups.
  • Eliot — Xing Yi Quan. A young prodigy with linear but powerful strikes and a unique forward-moving offense.
  • Lisa Hamilton (La Mariposa) — Lucha Libre. A high-flying wrestler with aerial throws, deceptive movement, and flashy ring-out combos.
  • Rig — Taekwondo. A kick-focused fighter with long reach, excellent keep-out tools, and tricky flamingo stance transitions.
  • Mila — Mixed Martial Arts (MMA). A ground-based fighter with takedowns, mounted strikes, and a realistic MMA moveset.
  • Marie Rose — Systema. A small-frame defensive specialist with parries, holds, and a unique "ronde" (spin) evasion mechanic.
  • Honoka — Honoka Fu (copy-style). A wildcard who copies moves from other characters, making her unpredictable but requiring matchup knowledge.
  • NiCO — Pencak Silat + Lightning Science. A tech-based fighter with electric-enhanced strikes and a unique teleport dash.
  • Diego — Street Fighting. A raw brawler with simple but hard-hitting combos — excellent for beginners who want immediate impact.

How to Unlock Characters in Original DOA6

If you are still playing the original DOA6 (non-Last Round), here is exactly how character unlocking worked in that version. This information is useful for players who bought the base game at launch and are wondering whether to upgrade to Last Round.

Phase 4 — Free Unlock via Story Mode

Phase 4 is the only character in the original DOA6 that can be unlocked without spending money. To unlock her:

  1. Go to Story Mode from the main menu
  2. Complete all 12 main story episodes (the numbered chapters, not the side-character episodes)
  3. After the credits roll and you return to the main menu, Phase 4 will appear on the Character Select screen
  4. Alternatively, she can be purchased directly from the platform store (PSN, Xbox Store, Steam) for approximately $3.99 USD
SPEEDRUN TIP: A full Story Mode playthrough takes approximately 2-3 hours on Normal difficulty. Skip cutscenes to reduce this to roughly 90 minutes. Set difficulty to "ROOKIE" for the fastest clear — you only need to complete the episodes, not achieve high ranks.

DLC Fighters — Purchase Required

The remaining six DLC characters (Mai, Kula, Tamaki, Momiji, Rachel, Nyotengu) must be purchased in the original DOA6. Options include:

  • Individual character purchase: $3.99 — $5.99 USD each
  • Season Pass bundles: Each Season Pass (1-4) includes 1-2 characters plus costumes, priced at $19.99 — $39.99 USD at launch
  • Complete Edition upgrade: Some platform stores offer a deluxe upgrade that bundles all DLC at a discount, though availability varies by region

Our recommendation: If you own the original DOA6 and want all characters, upgrading to DOA6 Last Round is typically more cost-effective than buying the DLC individually. The Last Round edition frequently goes on sale and includes all characters, stages, and a large costume catalog in one purchase. Check our DOA6 Price Guide for current deals across all platforms.

Core Fighters Version: What's Different

DOA6 also has a free-to-play edition called Core Fighters, and its unlock system is fundamentally different from both the original and Last Round editions:

  • Only 4 base characters are available for free: Kasumi, Hitomi, Diego, and Bass (rotation may vary by platform)
  • All other characters must be purchased individually — there is no "unlock by playing" mechanic in Core Fighters
  • Characters purchased in Core Fighters carry over if you later buy the full game on the same platform account
  • Story Mode is limited to the prologue chapters only; full Story Mode requires purchasing the Story Unlock Key
  • Online play is fully functional with your owned characters — you can queu ranked matches and lobbies without buying the full game
IS CORE FIGHTERS WORTH IT? Core Fighters is a great way to try DOA6 for free and test whether you enjoy the combat system. However, unlocking the full 31-character roster through individual purchases in Core Fighters would cost approximately $100+ USD — significantly more than buying DOA6 Last Round outright. Treat Core Fighters as a demo, and if you enjoy the game, upgrade to Last Round.

Character Select Screen: What to Expect

When you first open the Character Select screen in DOA6 Last Round, here is what you will see:

  • All 31 character icons are visible and selectable — no lock symbols, no dimmed-out portraits
  • Characters are arranged in a grid organized roughly by fighting style and series history
  • Each portrait shows the character's name and their default costume
  • Pressing left/right on a selected character cycles through their available costumes (which do need to be unlocked — see our Costumes Guide)
  • Random Select (the dice icon) is available from the start and randomly picks from all 31 fighters
  • Online mode only restricts characters you have not purchased — but in Last Round, you own all of them, so this is never an issue

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are all characters unlocked from the start in DOA6 Last Round?
Yes. DOA6 Last Round is the complete edition of the game, and all 31 characters — including the original 24 base fighters and all 7 DLC characters (Mai Shiranui, Kula Diamond, Tamaki, Momiji, Rachel, Phase 4, and Nyotengu) — are available from the moment you launch the game. There are no unlock requirements for any character in DOA6 Last Round.
Q: How do I unlock Phase 4 in the original DOA6?
In the original DOA6, Phase 4 could be unlocked by completing the main Story Mode campaign (all 12 episodes). Alternatively, she could be purchased individually or as part of the Season Pass. In DOA6 Last Round, Phase 4 is available immediately without any unlock requirements.
Q: How many characters are in DOA6 Last Round?
DOA6 Last Round features a total of 31 playable characters: 24 base roster fighters plus 7 DLC fighters. The DLC roster includes two KOF guest characters (Mai Shiranui and Kula Diamond) and five returning DOA characters (Tamaki, Momiji, Rachel, Phase 4, and Nyotengu), all included at no extra cost in the Last Round edition.
Q: Are there any secret characters in DOA6 Last Round?
No. DOA6 Last Round does not have any secret or hidden characters that require special unlock conditions. Every fighter in the game is visible on the character select screen from the start. The concept of "secret characters" was more common in earlier DOA titles like DOA2 and DOA3, but DOA6 Last Round makes the full roster transparent and immediately accessible.
Q: Do I need to buy DLC to get all characters in DOA6 Last Round?
No. DOA6 Last Round includes all 7 DLC characters as part of the base purchase. You do not need to buy any Season Passes or individual character DLC. The Last Round edition is specifically designed as the definitive, complete version that bundles everything together.
Q: Can I unlock characters by playing DOA Quest mode?
In the original DOA6, completing certain DOA Quest missions could unlock costume parts and in-game currency, but characters themselves were not unlockable through DOA Quest — only through Story Mode completion (Phase 4) or DLC purchase. In DOA6 Last Round, all characters are pre-unlocked, so DOA Quest is purely for earning costume patterns and Player Points.
Q: Is the Core Fighters version the same as Last Round for character unlocks?
No. Core Fighters is a free-to-play version that only includes 4 characters (Kasumi, Hitomi, Diego, Bass). You must purchase every other character individually. DOA6 Last Round is the paid complete edition with all 31 characters included. If you own Core Fighters and want the full roster, buying Last Round is much cheaper than purchasing characters one by one.
