How We Rank Characters

Our Dead or Alive 6 tier list is based on several factors drawn from the game's competitive scene and the character data in this guide:

  • Neutral strength: How well does the character control space and win the first exchange?
  • Combo damage: Can the character convert hits into meaningful damage and wall carry?
  • Mix-up potential: Does the character force the opponent to guess between multiple options?
  • Defensive tools: How well can the character escape pressure, reversal holds, and survive bad situations?
  • Matchup spread: How many characters in the roster give this fighter trouble?
  • Ease of use: How much execution and matchup knowledge does the character need to perform well?

Tier lists are never final. Balance patches, new strategies, and player innovation can shift rankings over time. Use this as a starting point, not a final verdict.

S-Tier (Top Tier)

Best-in-class tools with few bad matchups. Strong neutral, high combo damage, and excellent defensive options.

Kasumi — Mugen Tenshin Ninjutsu

Play Kasumi as a fast mid-range pest who chips away with quick checks, then explodes once you read a mistake.

Hayabusa — Hayabusa Ninjutsu

Build fear with fast pokes and throws, then cash out big once the opponent starts guessing wrong.

Ayane — Tenshin Ninjutsu

Frustrate the opponent with movement and stance transitions, then convert small wins into big knockdowns.

Marie Rose — Systema

Play for volume. Rack up small wins and wrong guesses until the round snowballs.

A-Tier (High Tier)

Well-rounded kits with one or two slightly unfavorable matchups. Highly competitive and solid tournament picks.

Jann Lee — Jeet Kune Do

Control the pace, make them press at the wrong time, and convert their mistakes into big damage.

Hitomi — Karate

Play honest, clean DOA. Make them guess in small pieces and snowball off stable confirms.

Tina — Pro Wrestling

Make them afraid to block, then make them afraid to press. Once they freeze, the throw game opens up.

Bayman — Combat Sambo

Play patient, trade smart, and make every opening as expensive as possible.

Rig — Taekwondo

Use range and tempo to control the fight, then launch when the opponent mistimes their defense.

Helena — Pi Qua Quan

Layer your offense until the opponent stops pressing, then open them up with throws and delayed hits.

Hayate — Mugen Tenshin Ninjutsu

Play a solid neutral game, clip them with good starters, and carry them into wall pressure.

Lisa — La Mariposa

Use flair to create hesitation, then force them into throw/launcher decisions they do not want to make.

Alpha-152 — Modified Mugen Tenshin

Take smart swings and make every opening as dramatic as possible. When you are ahead, press hard.

B-Tier (Mid Tier)

Viable characters requiring more effort to succeed. Specific strengths offset by notable weaknesses. Rewarding for dedicated mains.

Zack — Muay Thai

Break their timing. Swing when they expect silence, and stall when they expect aggression.

Bass — Pro Wrestling

Force respect with raw power, then punish hesitation with throws and big reads.

Mila — MMA

Play a practical MMA game: jab, wrestle, wall pin, and convert short bursts into big rounds.

Christie — She Quan

Make them uncomfortable with movement and stance pressure, then strike when they try to interrupt late.

Kokoro — Ba Ji Quan

Play a clean and efficient game where every hit builds toward a bigger confirm.

Eliot — Xing Yi Quan

Play tight, punish clean, and build rounds from simple advantages rather than big gambles.

Leifang — Tai Chi Quan

Let the opponent make the first mistake, then turn every wrong guess into real damage.

Brad Wong — Drunken Fist

Play the rhythm game. Lure swings, miss-callouts, and capitalize when they guess wrong.

Leon — Combat Sambo

Play a pressure game built on respect. Once they hesitate, crush them with heavy starters.

  • Best defensive: Helena, Leifang
  • Best evasive: Ayane, Marie Rose, Christie, Brad Wong
  • Best all-round beginner: Hitomi, Hayate, Eliot
  • Best high-damage specialist: Hayabusa, Alpha-152, Leon

Best Beginner Picks

New to DOA6 Last Round? Start with one of these four characters:

  1. Hitomi — The most beginner-friendly character in the game. Straightforward karate combos, balanced stats, and intuitive spacing.
  2. Kasumi — Fast and forgiving. Great for learning offensive pressure and whiff punishment.
  3. Jann Lee — Teaches counter-hit timing and spacing discipline with high reward.
  4. Tina — Teaches the throw game and wall positioning with devastating command grabs.

The most important factor is not tier placement — it is whether you enjoy the character enough to keep practicing. A player who loves their main will almost always outperform someone who copied a meta pick without understanding it.

Dead or Alive 6 Tier List FAQ

Is this tier list for DOA6 Last Round or the original DOA6?
This tier list is for DOA6 Last Round, the definitive edition that includes all DLC characters, stages, and the latest balance patches.
Who is the best character in Dead or Alive 6?
Kasumi and Hayabusa are generally considered the strongest all-around characters. Kasumi excels at speed and whiff punishment; Hayabusa excels at explosive mix-ups and throw damage. However, the best character for you depends on your playstyle.
Are B-tier characters viable in DOA6?
Absolutely. DOA6 Last Round is well-balanced enough that any character can win at most levels of play. B-tier characters simply require more effort or matchup knowledge to reach the same results as higher-tier picks.
How often does the DOA6 tier list change?
Tier lists shift when balance patches drop or when the community discovers new tech. We update this page as the meta evolves, so check back periodically for the latest rankings.
