How Many Costumes Are in DOA6 Last Round?

Dead or Alive has always been known for its extensive costume selection, and DOA6 Last Round takes that tradition to the extreme. The Last Round edition bundles every costume from the base game, all seasonal updates, and every DLC pack released during the game live-service period. The result is a staggering collection of over 500 costumes spread across the full 30+ character roster.

Each character has between 10 and 30+ costumes depending on their popularity and DLC treatment. Main characters like Kasumi, Ayane, and Marie Rose have the largest collections, while some DLC-exclusive characters have smaller but still impressive selections. The costumes range from faithful recreations of classic arcade outfits to entirely new designs, themed seasonal wear, and crossover costumes from other KOEI TECMO franchises.

The good news for free-to-play players is that a significant portion of these costumes can be unlocked entirely through gameplay. The base game alone includes dozens of unlockable outfits earned through Story Mode, DOA Quest, and the in-game shop. The DLC packs add hundreds more for players who want the complete wardrobe.

Free Unlock Methods: Story Mode and DOA Quest

The most rewarding free methods for unlocking costumes are Story Mode and DOA Quest. Both modes reward you with costumes and Player Points that you can spend in the shop.

Story Mode Costume Unlocks

Story Mode in DOA6 Last Round is divided into character-specific chapters. Completing individual chapters rewards you with costumes for the featured character. Each chapter typically unlocks one to three costumes, and some chapters have hidden rewards for completing bonus objectives.

Here is how Story Mode costume unlocking works:

  • Complete each chapter to earn the main costume unlock for that character.
  • Finish all chapters on higher difficulties to unlock additional color variations and alternate outfits.
  • Watch all cutscenes — some costumes are tied to story completion rather than just finishing fights.
  • Clean-up missions after the main story unlock bonus costumes for characters who appear in multiple chapters.

Story Mode is the best starting point because it introduces you to multiple characters while earning costumes. You will naturally unlock outfits for characters you might not have tried otherwise, expanding both your wardrobe and your character knowledge.

DOA Quest Costume Unlocks

DOA Quest is a mission-based mode where you complete specific objectives in controlled fight scenarios. Each quest rewards you with Player Points, and some quests directly unlock costumes or costume pieces. The quests range from simple win conditions to complex challenges that test specific mechanics.

Quest rewards are structured in tiers:

  • Basic quests reward small amounts of PP and common costume colors.
  • Intermediate quests reward larger PP amounts and unlock alternate costumes.
  • Advanced quests reward the highest PP payouts and rare costume unlocks that cannot be obtained elsewhere.
  • Completion bonuses — finishing all quests in a category grants a special costume or large PP bonus.

DOA Quest is the single most efficient free method for unlocking costumes because it rewards both direct costume unlocks and the currency needed to buy more from the shop. Prioritize clearing every quest if your goal is a complete wardrobe.

The In-Game Costume Shop: PP and OP Explained

The Costume Shop is where you spend earned and premium currency to purchase costumes directly. Understanding the two currency types is essential for efficient unlocking.

Player Points (PP)

Player Points (PP) are the free currency earned by playing any game mode. Every match you play, whether online ranked, local versus, Story Mode, or Training Mode, earns PP. The amount varies based on match length, difficulty, and performance, but you will accumulate substantial PP over time through regular play.

PP can purchase the majority of base-game costumes in the shop. Prices range from a few hundred PP for simple color swaps to several thousand PP for complete alternate outfits. Since PP is earned passively, you will unlock costumes naturally as you play — but if you want to speed up the process, there are specific farming methods covered later in this guide.

Owner Points (OP)

Owner Points (OP) are the premium currency. OP can be purchased with real money through your platform store, and some amounts are also earned through specific in-game achievements and milestone rewards. OP purchases premium costumes and special items that are not available for PP.

OP pricing varies by platform and region, but typical bundles range from small amounts for individual costumes to larger bundles for costume sets. The Last Round edition includes some OP bonuses that the base game did not, so check your balance before purchasing additional currency.

Shop Rotation and Availability

The costume shop inventory is not always static. Some costumes appear on rotation, meaning they are only available for purchase during certain periods. If you see a costume you want in the shop, purchase it while it is available. Rotating costumes typically return eventually, but the wait can be weeks or months.

The shop also features discounted costume bundles during seasonal sales and special events. These bundles offer multiple costumes at a lower combined price than buying them individually. If you plan to collect costumes for multiple characters, waiting for a bundle sale is the most cost-effective strategy.

Popular DLC Costume Themes

The DLC catalog covers a wide range of themes that have been released over the game lifetime:

  • Revival costumes — Classic outfits from DOA1 through DOA5, faithfully recreated for DOA6. Nostalgia-heavy picks for series veterans.
  • Seasonal costumes — Summer beach sets, Halloween costumes, Christmas outfits, and Valentine themed gear. Released during their respective seasons.
  • Collaboration costumes — Crossover outfits from other KOEI TECMO properties and guest franchises. These are among the rarest and most sought-after costumes.
  • Wedding costumes — Elegant formal wear for the full roster. One of the most popular DLC themes in the franchise.
  • High Society and Street costumes — Fashion-forward casual and formal outfits that give characters a completely different visual identity.
  • Happy Wedding and Design Contest costumes — Community-inspired designs and special themed collections that are unique to DOA6.

Are DLC Costumes Worth It?

This depends on your goals. If you play casually and are happy with the free unlockable costumes, the base game provides plenty of variety. If you are a dedicated player who wants visual variety for your main character, a character-specific DLC pack offers good value. If you are a collector who wants every costume, the Complete DLC Bundle during a seasonal sale is the most cost-effective way to get everything.

The Last Round edition includes some DLC costumes that were originally separate purchases in the base DOA6 release, so check what you already own before buying additional packs.

Best Methods for Farming Player Points

If you want to unlock costumes through the shop without spending real money, efficient PP farming is essential. Here are the best methods ranked by efficiency.

Method 1: DOA Quest Completion

DOA Quest missions reward the highest PP per time invested. Each completed quest gives a fixed PP reward, and clearing entire quest lines gives completion bonuses. If you have not finished all DOA Quest missions, this is your top priority. The PP rewards are front-loaded, meaning the first few hours of quest completion yield the most currency.

Method 2: Online Ranked Matches

Ranked matches reward more PP per match than any other standard mode. Longer matches and winning streaks increase the PP payout. If you enjoy competitive play, ranked matches are the most natural way to accumulate PP while improving your skills. Even losses earn PP, so there is no penalty for grinding ranked specifically for currency.

Method 3: Survival Mode on Hard

Survival Mode on Hard difficulty rewards substantial PP for each stage cleared. A skilled player can clear 20+ stages in a single run, earning a large PP payout in a concentrated session. This method is best for intermediate players who can reliably beat the AI on higher difficulties.

Method 4: Versus Mode AI Grinding

Setting up quick Versus matches against the AI on the easiest difficulty and winning rapidly is the simplest farming method. While the per-match PP reward is lower than ranked or survival, the matches are fast and you can farm passively while watching videos or doing other activities. Set rounds to one and the time limit to the minimum for maximum efficiency.

Method 5: Combo Challenge Completion

Completing combo challenges for every character rewards PP and occasionally unlocks costume items. This method serves double duty — you learn character combos while earning currency. If you plan to experiment with multiple characters anyway, this is a productive use of your time.

Rare and Limited-Edition Costumes

Some costumes in DOA6 Last Round are more difficult to obtain than others. Understanding which costumes are rare helps you prioritize your unlocking efforts.

Story Mode Exclusive Costumes

Certain costumes are only available through Story Mode and cannot be purchased in the shop. These are tied to specific chapter completions and are missable if you do not complete the right chapters. Make sure to finish every Story Mode chapter at least once to claim these exclusive rewards.

High-PP Shop Costumes

The most expensive costumes in the shop cost thousands of PP and represent the ultimate free-to-play unlock goal. These are typically alternate versions of popular costumes with unique color schemes and design elements. They exist as long-term goals for dedicated players.

Seasonal Event Costumes

Costumes released during seasonal events (Halloween, Christmas, Summer) may only be available during their event window. While these costumes sometimes return during the following year event, there is no guarantee. If you see a seasonal costume you want, unlock or purchase it during the event period.

Pre-Order and Special Edition Costumes

Players who pre-ordered DOA6 or purchased specific editions received exclusive costumes that are not available through any other method. The Last Round edition includes some of these previously exclusive costumes, but a few remain locked to original pre-order codes. These are the rarest costumes in the game and are only obtainable through code redemption if unused codes still exist.

Costume Unlock Tips and Tricks

Maximize your costume unlock efficiency with these practical tips:

  1. Start with Story Mode. It gives you free costumes and introduces you to the roster. Complete every chapter before focusing on other methods.
  2. Prioritize DOA Quest. The PP rewards from quests are the highest you can earn without DLC. Clear every quest for maximum currency and direct costume unlocks.
  3. Focus on your main first. Instead of spreading PP across the entire roster, buy all costumes for your main character before expanding to others. This gives you the most visual variety for the character you play most.
  4. Watch for sales. DLC costume packs go on sale during platform-wide sales events. PlayStation Store, Xbox Store, and Steam all run periodic discounts. A seasonal sale can save you 30-50% on costume bundles.
  5. Check the shop regularly. Rotating inventory means costumes come and go. Visit the shop after each play session to see if anything new or desirable appeared.
  6. Complete daily challenges. If the game offers daily or weekly challenges, these often reward bonus PP. Small amounts add up over time.
  7. Use combo challenges as warm-ups. Instead of spending 10 minutes warming up in Training Mode, run through combo challenges. You earn PP and practice execution simultaneously.
  8. Consider the Complete DLC Bundle. If you plan to buy multiple individual DLC packs, calculate whether the Complete Bundle is cheaper. During sales, the bundle is almost always the better deal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you unlock costumes in DOA6 Last Round?
Costumes can be unlocked through Story Mode chapter completions, DOA Quest mission rewards, the in-game costume shop using Player Points (PP) and Owner Points (OP), and by purchasing DLC costume packs from your platform store.
How many costumes does DOA6 Last Round have?
Over 500 costumes across all characters when counting base game defaults, unlockable outfits, and DLC costume packs. Main characters like Kasumi and Ayane have 30 or more costumes each.
What are Player Points and Owner Points?
Player Points (PP) are free currency earned by playing any game mode, used to buy most costumes in the shop. Owner Points (OP) are premium currency purchased with real money or earned through specific achievements, used for exclusive premium costumes.
Can you unlock all costumes without buying DLC?
You can unlock many base-game costumes through Story Mode, DOA Quest, and the PP shop without spending real money. However, DLC-exclusive costume packs containing themed sets and collaboration costumes require separate purchases.
What is the fastest way to unlock costumes?
Complete all DOA Quest missions first for direct costume unlocks and large PP rewards. Then play online ranked matches for the highest PP per match. Combining both methods is the fastest path to a complete wardrobe.
Do DLC costumes give competitive advantages?
No. Costumes in DOA6 Last Round are purely cosmetic and do not affect gameplay, stats, hitboxes, or frame data. They change only the visual appearance of your character.
Does Last Round include costumes from the base DOA6?
Yes. Last Round is the definitive edition that bundles all content from the base game and its live-service period, including many costumes that were originally separate DLC purchases.
Are there costumes exclusive to specific platforms?
The vast majority of costumes are available across all platforms. Some pre-order bonuses or platform-specific promotional costumes may be exclusive, but Last Round has reduced these differences significantly.
