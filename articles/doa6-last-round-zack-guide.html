DOA6 Last Round Zack Guide: Combos, Frame Data & Complete Strategy for Mastering the DJ Capoeira Fighter

Zack is Dead or Alive's wildcard — a DJ, a showman, and a surprisingly deep fighter wrapped in the flashiest moveset on the roster. This guide covers his complete toolkit: from his essential pokes and optimal combo routes to his Capoeira stance mix-ups, Ducking stance pressure, wall carry sequences, Break Blow extensions, okizeme setups, and matchup-specific strategies that will help you climb ranked with style.

Zack Overview: Playstyle, Strengths & Weaknesses

Zack is a mix-up oriented mid-range character who thrives on unpredictability. Unlike Kasumi's linear rushdown or Hayabusa's explosive one-shot damage, Zack's game plan revolves around conditioning and reads. He uses his two signature stances — Capoeira and Ducking — to create layered mix-ups that force opponents into guessing games at every interaction. His Capoeira stance enters from multiple moves and offers mid/low/throw options, while Ducking stance evades highs and gives access to fast launchers and pokes.

Strengths

  • Best sidestep attack in the game: 236P (Ducking~P) is a fully invincible sidestep launcher that crushes highs and mids. It punishes linear offense from any character and converts into full combos on hit.
  • Unpredictable stance mix-ups: Capoeira stance with mid/low/throw options creates genuine 50/50 situations at close range. Opponents must guess correctly or eat 80+ damage.
  • Strong okizeme: Zack's knockdown pressure is among the best in DOA6. After a knockdown, his Capoeira~K (low meaty), Capoeira~T (throw on wake-up), and delayed Capoeira~P (mid catch) create a three-way mix that is extremely difficult to escape.
  • High style ceiling: Zack's diverse moveset means no two Zack players fight the same. His creative combo routes and stance transitions give him a unique psychological advantage — opponents cannot auto-pilot against him.

Weaknesses

  • Inconsistent damage on heavyweights: Several of Zack's optimal juggle routes drop on Bass and Bayman. You need separate, lower-damage routes for heavy characters.
  • Average jab speed: At 10 frames, Zack's P is slower than Kasumi (9f), Ayane (9f), and Christie (10f). He loses mash wars against the fastest characters.
  • Stance vulnerability: Both Capoeira and Ducking stances have startup frames where Zack is vulnerable. Opponents who recognize the stance entry can interrupt with fast mids.
  • Below-average health: 950 HP means Zack cannot afford to trade hits with power characters. He needs to win the neutral game cleanly rather than absorbing damage.
PRO TIP: Zack's biggest advantage is psychological. Most players have limited matchup experience against a good Zack because he is rarely seen in tournaments. Use this to your advantage — lean into unconventional options, stance cancels, and timing variations that opponents have not labbed against. The first game of any set is where Zack shines brightest because the opponent is learning your patterns in real time.

Wall Carry & Wall Combo Optimization

Zack's wall carry is average — he lacks the explosive forward momentum of Kasumi's 6PK or Hayabusa's wall-seeking strings. However, his wall combo damage is solid once he gets the splat. The key is stage awareness — knowing when to go for wall carry versus when to take midscreen damage.

Universal Wall Carry Template

Zack's wall combo follows: launcher → carry string → splat move → wall ender. His carry string is 6KK (two-hit kick with horizontal push) plus forward-dash adjustments. The splat move is typically the second hit of 6KK or PP6K when close to the wall.

Stage-Specific Tips

  • The Throwdown (Octagon): Walls on all sides — Zack's best stage for wall combos. Launch from any position and you will reach a wall within 3-4 hits. Default to your wall route here.
  • Zero (Lab): Close walls plus electric floors. Electric floor launches give bonus damage and a second wall splat opportunity. Prioritize wall combos on this stage.
  • Forbidden Fortune (Ship): Side rails are close. 6KK naturally carries toward rails. Break Blow can send opponents over the rail for a stage transition.
  • Lost Paradise (Desert): Open stage with distant walls. Do not fish for wall combos — use midscreen routes and save Break Blow for guaranteed damage.
WALL AWARENESS DRILL: Load Training Mode on The Throwdown. Start center stage. Launch with 33P and practice the carry: 33P → 6KK → PP6K → 6KKP. Count how many hits it takes to reach the wall from each starting position (center, half-wall, near-wall). Knowing your "carry count" per launcher per stage is what separates intermediate Zack players from advanced ones.

Break Blow & Break Hold Zack-Specific Routes

Zack's Break Blow is a solid combo extender that bumps his damage from the 80-95 range to the 130-145 range. The Break Hold (4S) gives him a universal defensive reversal that compensates for his below-average health.

Break Blow Wall Extension (Optimal)

The sequence: 33P (launcher) → 6S (Break Blow, hold S to charge) → opponent hits wall (wall splat) → press H to cancel → PP6K → 6KKP. Total damage: ~135. This is Zack's highest-damage route and should be your default whenever you have full meter near a wall.

Cancel timing: press H roughly 15 frames after the wall impact sound. Too early and the splat does not register; too late and the opponent slides down before your follow-up connects. In Training Mode, set Critical Gauge to FULL and practice until you can land 10 consecutive cancel extensions.

Break Hold Applications

Zack's Break Hold (4S) is a universal parry that beats all strike levels. Use it in these scenarios:

  • Against predictable meaty pressure: When opponents run the same wake-up attack pattern, Break Hold on wake-up steals momentum.
  • During string gaps: Characters like Hitomi and Jann Lee have delayable strings. If you recognize the gap, Break Hold during the delay window.
  • As a round-ender: If the opponent is low health, raw Break Hold into Break Blow can surprise-kill. The animation is fast enough to catch many players off guard.
METER PRIORITY: As Zack, prioritize Break Blow for offensive combos over Break Hold defensive reads. Zack's damage without meter is below average — meter spent on Break Blow wall extensions closes rounds faster. Only use Break Hold when you have a hard read on a specific pattern or when you are at critical health and need to survive.

Okizeme: Zack's Knockdown Pressure Game

This is where Zack truly separates himself from the cast. After every knockdown, Zack has access to one of the most oppressive okizeme games in DOA6 Last Round. His Capoeira stance transitions create a genuine three-way mix-up that opponents must guess on every wake-up.

The Core Okizeme Sequence

After any knockdown (combo ender, throw, or sweep), immediately enter Capoeira stance and position yourself at the opponent's wake-up range. You now have three options:

  1. Capoeira~K (low sweep): Times with the opponent's wake-up for a meaty low. On counter-hit, launches for a full combo (~90 damage). This is your highest-reward oki option and should be used most frequently.
  2. Capoeira~T (command throw): Catches opponents who stand up blocking. 55 damage plus side-switch. Use when the opponent is conditioned to block the low after repeated Capoeira~K knockdowns.
  3. Capoeira~P (mid poke): Safe mid option that catches opponents trying to sidestep the low or throw. Deals modest damage but maintains pressure and resets the knockdown situation.

Advanced Okizeme: Delayed Timing

The true power of Zack's okizeme is timing variance. Instead of attacking immediately on the opponent's wake-up frame, sometimes delay your Capoeira follow-up by 100-200ms. This catches opponents who try to:

  • Wake-up attack: Their attack whiffs through your delayed timing, and you punish during their recovery.
  • Wake-up sidestep: Delayed Capoeira~P catches sidesteps because it tracks better with the extra frames.
  • Wake-up hold: If they hold expecting a meaty, your delay causes the hold to whiff, and you get a free throw or launcher.
OKI SEQUENCE AFTER THROW: After Zack's 46T command throw (which side-switches), immediately input 214P+K to enter Capoeira. The side-switch disorients the opponent's directional inputs, making your Capoeira low even harder to block correctly. This throw-into-Capoeira loop is Zack's signature vortex and will carry you through intermediate ranks.

Matchup Strategy: How to Fight Every Archetype

Zack's matchup spread is generally even across the board. His unpredictability means he has no truly unwinnable matchups, but he also has no free wins. Success depends on your ability to adapt your stance usage to each opponent.

Vs. Speed Characters (Kasumi, Ayane, Christie, Hitomi)

Even to slightly unfavorable. These characters have faster jabs than Zack's 10-frame P, meaning they win close-range scrambles. The game plan: use Ducking~P (236P) to punish their linear rushdown and create space. Do not try to out-poke Kasumi at close range — her 9-frame jab will beat yours every time. Instead, play at mid-range where Zack's stances are harder to interrupt and use 236P to sidestep their approach tools.

Against Ayane specifically: her spinning evasion makes 236P less effective since her spins can evade your sidestep launch. In this matchup, favor Capoeira~T (throw) more than usual — Ayane players who spin frequently are vulnerable to throws during spin recovery.

Vs. Grapplers (Bass, Tina, Leon, Bayman)

Slightly favorable. Grapplers struggle with Zack's Ducking stance evasion and mid-range stance mix-ups. The game plan: never stay at close range long enough for their command throws. Use Capoeira stance from mid-range to create mix-ups without entering their throw range. Key move: Capoeira~P (safe mid) into backdash. Poke with the safe mid, retreat, and reset. Against heavyweights specifically, use your weight-class-adjusted combos (6KK → PKK enders) to avoid drops.

Vs. All-Rounders (Hayabusa, Jann Lee, Rig, Diego)

Even. These matchups are decided by who reads the opponent better. Against Hayabusa, respect his Izuna Drop — do not Ducking into his throw range. Against Jann Lee, his Dragon Kick (66K) will catch Capoeira stance entries, so use stance cancels to bait it. Against Diego, his pressure strings are straightforward — learn the gaps and use 236P to punish predictable sequences.

Vs. Zoners (Raidou, Nyotengu, Helena)

Favorable. Zack's Ducking stance crushes high projectiles and approach tools. Use 236 to close distance while evading their zoning, then transition into Capoeira mix-ups at close range. Against Helena specifically, her Bokuho stance and Zack's Capoeira create an interesting stance-vs-stance dynamic — favor Capoeira~K (low) since Helena players tend to stand-block from Bokuho.

MATCHUP ADAPTATION: After every ranked session, note which Capoeira follow-up your opponent struggled with most. If they could not block the low, keep using Capoeira~K. If they froze on Capoeira stance entry, use Capoeira~T. If they tried to interrupt your stance, use stance cancel → 236P to sidestep their interrupt attempt and launch. This adaptive loop is what makes Zack viable against any character.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Zack good in DOA6 Last Round?
Zack is a solid mid-tier character in DOA6 Last Round, typically ranked B+ to A-tier. His strengths lie in his unpredictable mix-up game, unique Capoeira stance transitions, strong okizeme pressure, and one of the best sidestep attacks in the game (236P). His main weaknesses are below-average health (950 HP), inconsistent combo damage on heavier characters, and a reliance on reads over guaranteed setups. Zack rewards creative, adaptive players who excel at conditioning opponents and capitalizing on mental momentum. He sees occasional tournament representation and is particularly effective in online ranked where opponents have limited matchup experience against him.
Q: What are Zack's best combo starters in DOA6?
Zack's best combo starters are 33P (16-frame mid launcher, safe on block at -7 — your go-to launcher), 236P (16-frame sidestep launcher with full invincibility — your defensive punish tool), 66K (17-frame mid launcher with forward momentum for wall carry), and 4H+K (19-frame low-crushing launcher for beating throws and lows). For counter-hit fishing, his 6P (12-frame mid) and 3K (14-frame low) are the buttons you will press most in neutral. From Ducking stance, Ducking~K is a fast low that launches on counter-hit for full combos.
Q: How do I use Zack's Capoeira stance effectively?
Zack's Capoeira stance is his signature mix-up tool with four follow-ups: Capoeira~P (safe mid, +1 on hit, -3 on block), Capoeira~K (low sweep, full combo on counter-hit, -15 on block), Capoeira~T (command throw, 55 damage, side-switches), and Capoeira~P+K (overhead, ground-bounces for juggle extensions). The key is entering Capoeira from safe string enders like 6KK→214 or PP6K→214, then varying your timing and follow-up selection. Delay your attack sometimes to create unreactable mix-ups. Use Capoeira~P as your safe default, Capoeira~K when the opponent blocks expecting the mid, and Capoeira~T when they freeze up seeing the stance.
Q: What is Zack's best wall combo route?
Zack's optimal wall combo on The Throwdown is 33P (launcher) → PP6K (wall carry) → K (wall splat) → 6KKP (wall ender), dealing approximately 115-125 damage. With full Break Gauge: 33P → Break Blow (hold S, wall splat) → cancel with H → PP6K → 6KKP for 135-145 damage. Zack's wall carry is average — use 6KK as the carry tool since the final hit has strong horizontal push. On stages with close walls like Zero, you can convert most hits into wall combos consistently. On open stages like Lost Paradise, take midscreen damage instead of fishing for wall carry.
Q: How do I deal with pressure characters as Zack?
Against rushdown characters like Kasumi and Christie, Zack's defensive game relies on three tools: 236P (sidestep attack with full invincibility — punishes linear rushdown for free), 2P (12-frame low jab for interrupting strings), and Break Hold (4S) for hard reads on predictable pressure sequences. The matchup plan: block the first hit of their pressure, identify the gap after their safe ender, and interrupt with 2P or sidestep with 236P. Against string-heavy characters like Hitomi, learn which strings are delayable and use the delay window to input Break Hold or sidestep. Do not try to out-poke Kasumi at close range — her 9-frame jab beats Zack's 10-frame P.
Q: What are Zack's weaknesses and bad matchups in DOA6?
Zack's main weaknesses are: below-average health (950 HP — dies faster than the 1000 HP standard), inconsistent damage on heavyweights (several combo routes drop on Bass/Bayman requiring separate lower-damage routes), average jab speed (10-frame P loses to Kasumi, Ayane, and Christie in close-range scrambles), and stance vulnerability (Capoeira and Ducking entry frames can be interrupted by fast mids if opponents recognize the animation). His worst matchups are against characters with fast jabs and strong pressure who can interrupt his stance entries: Kasumi (out-pokes him at close range), Christie (Her stance transitions outpace his), and Hayabusa (Izuna Drop punishes predictable Capoeira entries).
