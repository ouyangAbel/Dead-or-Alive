Every DOA6 DLC Character in Detail

1. Mai Shiranui — The Legendary Kunoichi (KOF Guest)

AttributeDetails
Fighting StyleShiranui-ryuu Ninjutsu
OriginSNK's Fatal Fury / King of Fighters series
Season PassSeason Pass 2
PlaystyleMid-range zoning with fan projectiles, strong aerial mobility, explosive close-range combos
DifficultyIntermediate

Mai Shiranui is the most recognizable guest character in DOA6. She brings her iconic fans, the Kachousen projectile, and acrobatic combos directly from King of Fighters. Mai excels at controlling space — her Hishou Ryuuenjin (rising flame attack) and Ryuuenbu (spinning fan attack) give her strong anti-air and whiff-punish tools. Her fan toss forces opponents to approach, which she punishes with fast strings and deceptive range.

Mai's transition to the DOA system is smooth: her KOF specials translate naturally into the Critical Stun system, and she builds Break Gauge quickly with her multi-hit fan strings. Players familiar with KOF will find her special move inputs (quarter-circle-forward + P/K) comfortable. She is a strong pick for players who enjoy controlling neutral and punishing impatient approaches.

2. Kula Diamond — The Ice Queen (KOF Guest)

AttributeDetails
Fighting StyleAnti-K' Arts (Ice-based combat)
OriginSNK's King of Fighters series
Season PassSeason Pass 2
PlaystyleAggressive rushdown with ice projectiles, fast pokes, and re-stand setups for extended pressure loops
DifficultyIntermediate-Advanced

Kula Diamond brings the opposite approach to Mai — while Mai controls range, Kula rushes it down. Her ice-based moves include Diamond Breath (ice projectile), Crow Bite (rising uppercut), and Lay Spin (sliding low). Her fast normals and special-cancel windows let her chain pressure in ways most DOA characters cannot match. Kula's re-stand combos are particularly dangerous: she can end juggles by planting the opponent back on their feet, forcing an immediate mix-up while they are still in disadvantage.

Kula's weakness is range — she struggles against characters that can keep her at mid-distance, and her defense relies heavily on system mechanics rather than character-specific tools. Pairing her with a strong Break Hold game is essential.

3. Tamaki — The Fashion Designer

AttributeDetails
Fighting StyleAikido
OriginDOA original (introduced in DOAXVV)
Season PassSeason Pass 3
PlaystyleDefensive counter-fighter with parries, circular throws, and heavy punish damage from defensive reads
DifficultyAdvanced

Tamaki is a pure defensive character built around Aikido principles — she uses the opponent's momentum against them. Her kit includes unique parry stances, circular throws that reposition both fighters, and high-damage punishes from correct defensive reads. Tamaki rewards patience and matchup knowledge. Her parries cover high, mid, and low attacks depending on the input, making her one of the few characters who can consistently punish pressure-heavy opponents with zero meter.

Tamaki is not beginner-friendly — her offense is limited, and she relies on forcing the opponent into mistakes. But in the right hands, she dismantles aggressive players and forces the match into a slow, deliberate pace where she holds the advantage.

4. Momiji — The Dragon Shrine Maiden

AttributeDetails
Fighting StyleHayabusa-ryuu Aikijutsu
OriginNinja Gaiden series
Season PassSeason Pass 4
PlaystyleVersatile all-rounder with double jumps, air throws, strong wall carry, and flexible combo routing
DifficultyBeginner-Friendly

Momiji returns from Dead or Alive 5 Last Round as a fan-favorite all-rounder. Trained by Ryu Hayabusa, she shares some ninja mobility but emphasizes aerial combat more heavily. Her signature double jump (Tenku) lets her extend air combos and reposition mid-juggle, while her air throw (Ukifune) punishes opponents who try to challenge her in the air. Momiji has strong wall carry thanks to her Izuna follow-ups and can convert almost any launcher into respectable damage.

Momiji is one of the best DLC characters for newcomers — her tools are intuitive, her combos are forgiving, and she does not rely on any single gimmick. She fits comfortably into any playstyle and rewards fundamental DOA skills.

5. Rachel — The Fiend Hunter

AttributeDetails
Fighting StyleCounter-Fiend Combat
OriginNinja Gaiden series
Season PassSeason Pass 4
PlaystyleHeavy-pressure brawler with stomp resets, hard knockdowns, and oppressive ground game
DifficultyIntermediate

Rachel is the powerhouse of the DLC cast. She fights with brutal, heavy strikes that emphasize hard knockdowns and ground pressure. Her signature stomp (Air Raid) can catch opponents on the ground and reset them into another mix-up. Rachel's damage output is among the highest in the game when she successfully loops knockdown-to-stomp sequences, and her simple but effective toolset makes her a threat in any matchup.

Rachel's weakness is speed — her attacks are slow to start, and she struggles against characters who can interrupt her pressure. Her defense relies on well-timed holds and Break Hold escapes. Players who enjoy Bass or Tina's grappling style will find Rachel a natural fit, but with more emphasis on strikes than throws.

6. Phase 4 — The Kasumi Clone

AttributeDetails
Fighting StyleMugen Tenshin Ninjutsu (Teleport variant)
OriginDOA original (Kasumi clone)
Season PassPre-order bonus / Season Pass 1
PlaystyleHigh-execution rushdown with teleport cancels, ambiguous mix-ups, and glass-cannon damage
DifficultyAdvanced

Phase 4 is Kasumi's darker clone and one of the most execution-heavy characters in DOA6. She shares Kasumi's speed and basic strings but adds teleport cancels (Chifu) that let her disappear mid-combo and reappear for extended pressure or repositioning. Her teleport mix-ups create ambiguous left/right situations that are very hard to block on reaction. Phase 4 also takes more damage than most characters, making her a true glass cannon — she deals enormous damage off a single opening but crumbles quickly under sustained pressure.

Phase 4 is a specialist's character. Her execution demands precise timing, and her defense requires system-level mastery since she has no character-specific defensive gimmicks. For players willing to invest the time, she is one of the scariest offensive characters in the game.

7. Nyotengu — The Tengu Princess

AttributeDetails
Fighting StyleTengu-do (Flight-based combat)
OriginDOA original (returning from DOA5)
Season PassPre-order bonus / Season Pass 1
PlaystyleZoning with flight stance, strong mid-range normals, tricky aerial mix-ups, and high-damage launchers
DifficultyIntermediate

Nyotengu is one of DOA's most unique fighters. Her Hiten (flight stance) lets her take to the air mid-combo, opening aerial mix-ups that few other characters can replicate. From flight, she can divekick, throw, or reposition — forcing the opponent to guess between multiple threats. Her long-reaching normals control mid-range well, and her launchers lead to high-damage juggles when paired with flight extensions.

Nyotengu's weakness is close-range defense — once an opponent gets inside her preferred range, she struggles to reset spacing without spending Break Gauge. She rewards players who understand spacing and can condition opponents to respect her range.

How to Unlock All Characters in DOA6 Last Round

One of the most common questions from new players: how do you unlock all characters? The answer for Last Round is simple — you do not need to do anything. Every character is available from the moment you launch the game. There are no story-mode unlocks, no in-game currency requirements, and no hidden conditions. Select any fighter from the character select screen and start playing.

If You Are Playing the Original DOA6 (Not Last Round)

  • Base characters (24): All unlocked from the start. Story Mode completion is not required for any base character in DOA6.
  • Mai Shiranui & Kula Diamond: Purchase Season Pass 2 or individual character DLC from your platform store.
  • Tamaki: Purchase Season Pass 3 or individual character DLC.
  • Momiji & Rachel: Purchase Season Pass 4 or individual character DLC.
  • Phase 4 & Nyotengu: Purchase Season Pass 1 or individual character DLC. Originally pre-order exclusives.
RECOMMENDATION: If you are playing the original DOA6 and want the full roster, upgrading to Last Round is significantly cheaper than buying individual characters or Season Passes. The Last Round edition is the complete package and the definitive way to play.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many DLC characters are in DOA6 Last Round?
DOA6 Last Round includes 7 DLC fighters added after the original launch: Mai Shiranui, Kula Diamond, Tamaki, Momiji, Rachel, Phase 4, and Nyotengu. Combined with the 24 base characters, the total roster is 31 fighters.
Are all DOA6 DLC characters free in Last Round?
Yes. DOA6 Last Round bundles all DLC characters and most costumes at no extra cost. You do not need to buy Season Passes or individual characters — everything is included from the first launch.
Who are the guest characters in DOA6?
Mai Shiranui and Kula Diamond from SNK's King of Fighters series are the two guest characters in DOA6. Both retain their signature KOF special moves adapted for the DOA fighting system, including fan projectiles (Mai) and ice attacks (Kula).
Does DOA6 Last Round have all characters unlocked from the start?
Yes. All 31 characters are available immediately — no story mode completion, no in-game currency farming, no hidden conditions. Select any character and play.
What is the difference between DOA6 and DOA6 Last Round roster?
DOA6 Last Round includes 7 additional characters that were DLC in the original game. The original DOA6 had 24 base characters; Last Round has 31. Last Round also includes most DLC costumes and runs on the final balance patch.
Is Mai Shiranui in DOA6 Last Round?
Yes, Mai Shiranui is fully playable in DOA6 Last Round. She was originally a Season Pass 2 DLC fighter in DOA6 but is included by default in the Last Round edition along with all her costumes.
How do you unlock Tamaki in DOA6?
In DOA6 Last Round, Tamaki is unlocked from the start. In the original DOA6, Tamaki was a Season Pass 3 DLC character requiring individual purchase from your platform's store.
Which DLC character is best for beginners?
Momiji is the most beginner-friendly DLC character. Her tools are intuitive, combos are forgiving, and she rewards fundamental DOA skills without relying on complex gimmicks. Mai Shiranui is also a solid choice for players comfortable with fighting game fundamentals.
Can you buy individual DLC characters for the original DOA6?
Yes, every DLC character was sold individually on each platform's store in addition to being part of Season Pass bundles. However, prices varied by platform and region, and some characters are no longer listed on certain stores. Upgrading to Last Round is the safest way to get the full roster.
