Strengths and Weaknesses

Core Strengths

  • huge throw damage
  • great stage carry
  • strong wall pressure
  • very scary after knockdown

These strengths define why TINA can compete at a high level.

Main Weaknesses

  • needs openings to cash out
  • committal under pressure
  • struggles against patient keep-out
  • less effective without stage position

Understanding weaknesses helps you build better practice habits.

Beginner Gameplan

If you are still learning TINA, start with a simple, repeatable gameplan.

  • safe string into throw attempt
  • launcher into simple juggle
  • basic wall finisher
  • poke into spacing reset

Advanced Strategy

At higher levels, TINA becomes more about timing control and layered reads.

  • command throw conditioning
  • launcher routing after stagger fear
  • stage-specific damage paths
  • delayed reset timing

Matchup Priorities

General advice: Use strikes to build hesitation, then drag them toward the wall where throws hurt most.

Character strategy: Make them afraid to block, then make them afraid to press. Once they freeze, the throw game opens up.
